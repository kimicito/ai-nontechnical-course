Level 16 — Compliance

16.4 Practical Summary

⏱ 15 min · level summary
Main Principle of Level 16
Compliance combines the sensitivity of internal audit (vulnerability map, whistleblower), legal (privilege, regulatory consequences), and security (person investigations). On-prem is the default rule.

Key Takeaways

1. On-Prem — Default Rule

For compliance, on-prem is not an exception but the norm:

  • Specific deals and counterparties — sanctions analysis, KYC/KYB, due diligence
  • Transactions and alerts — suspicious activity analysis, SAR/STR
  • Investigations — corruption, whistleblower reports, committee materials
  • Management reporting — compliance reports for board/audit committee
  • Regulatory requests — responses to inquiries, audit preparation
  • Risk assessment of real processes — company vulnerability map

2. External AI — Regulatory Field Only

Safe tasks for cloud:

  • Sanctions regime monitoring (public lists)
  • Export control conclusions (general norms)
  • Policy development based on public frameworks (ISO 37001, UK Bribery Act)
  • Code of business ethics (general structure)
  • Training programs (general content)
  • Benchmarking (public reports)

3. Hybrid — for Methodology

Three tasks work on hybrid scheme:

  • Sanctions screening — interpretation methodology outside, decision on name inside
  • Risk assessment — matrix and methodology outside, application to processes inside
  • Training scenarios — anonymized case outside, real case inside
  • Due diligence — public research outside, internal assessment inside

4. Unique Factor — The Fact of Checking is Confidential

Additional compliance specificity: any task where a specific person's or company's name appears at input or output, checked against sanctions/PEP status — is on-prem by default. The very fact of "who was checked and why" is sensitive information regardless of the check result.

What's Excluded from Level (and Why)

Automated screening against sanctions/PEP databases — rule-based or ML-matching systems (World-Check, Dow Jones Risk & Compliance). This is not generative AI but specialized software with database integration. GenAI is connected only to interpretation, prioritization, and formatting of results, not to the matching process itself.

Checklist for Compliance Officer Before External AI

Before sending request to external AI, check:

  • ❌ No counterparty company names?
  • ❌ No beneficiary/PEP names?
  • ❌ No specific transaction amounts?
  • ❌ No account numbers?
  • ❌ No deal dates?
  • ❌ No company department names?
  • ❌ No board strategic decisions?
  • ❌ No whistleblower report details?

If any item fails — on-prem only.
