14.2 External Tasks: External AI

⏱ 20 min · for marketers, SMM managers, content managers
Open Data (External AI)
All tasks in this lesson work with public content, open sources, general methodologies. You can use Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini.

Why Marketing is One of the Safest Zones for External AI

Marketing content is public by definition. The end product (post, brochure, ad copy) is intended for a wide audience. The risk of trade secret leakage is minimal — as long as you don't share internal budgets, prices, and strategic plans.

Important: before sending to the cloud — check that your request doesn't contain real prices, budgets, client data, or strategic plans. If it does — anonymize or send to on-prem.

1. Content Plan Development

Generating ideas, rubrics, publication calendar — native territory for genAI.

Prompt Example (Claude with Web Search)
Prepare a monthly content plan for a B2B company in [SPECIFY INDUSTRY]:

Parameters:
- Channels: LinkedIn, corporate blog, email newsletter
- Target audience: [SPECIFY: procurement, engineers, directors]
- Content blocks: expertise, cases, trends, product
- Frequency: 3 LinkedIn posts/week, 2 articles/month, 4 emails

Develop:
1. Topics for each post (headline + format: carousel, text, video)
2. Publication calendar
3. Hooks for each topic (what will grab attention)
4. Calls to action (CTA)
5. Cross-channel links

Result: ready content plan, saves 4–5 hours.

2. Advertising Copywriting

Generating headline variants, ad texts, descriptions — without real budgets.

Prompt Example
Write 5 ad variants for a B2B audience:

Product: [SPECIFY PRODUCT TYPE] for [SPECIFY INDUSTRY]
USP: [SPECIFY KEY ADVANTAGE]
Target: [SPECIFY POSITION], companies [SPECIFY SIZE]
Format: Google Ads (3 headlines x 30 chars, 2 descriptions x 90 chars)

Additional:
- 3 LinkedIn variants (more expert tone)
- 3 email newsletter variants (personalized)
- A/B test hypotheses: what to test (headline, CTA, offer)

Result: ad set for testing, saves 2–3 hours.

3. SEO Content Optimization

Generating meta descriptions, H1-H3 headlines, alt texts — public content.

Prompt Example
Optimize text for SEO:

Original text (blog article):
[PASTE ARTICLE TEXT]

Keywords (priority):
1. [SPECIFY KEYWORD 1]
2. [SPECIFY KEYWORD 2]
3. [SPECIFY KEYWORD 3]

Develop:
1. H1 headline (60 chars, with keyword)
2. Meta description (150 chars, with CTA)
3. H2-H3 structure (with keyword inclusion)
4. Alt texts for 3 images
5. Internal optimization checklist (cross-linking)

Result: optimized text, saves 1.5 hours.

4. Competitor Analysis from Open Data

Product line, positioning, public prices — all in open access.

Prompt Example (Web Search)
Conduct analysis of 3 competitors in [SPECIFY MARKET] segment:

Competitors:
1. [SPECIFY COMPANY 1]
2. [SPECIFY COMPANY 2]
3. [SPECIFY COMPANY 3]

Data to collect (public sources):
- Product line (categories, SKU)
- Positioning (how they position themselves)
- Public prices (if on website)
- Promotion channels (where they advertise)
- Strengths (from reviews, cases)
- Weaknesses (complaints, gaps)

Develop:
1. Comparison table
2. Positioning map (how they see themselves vs how clients see them)
3. White spaces (what competitors don't cover — our opportunity)
4. Differentiation recommendations

Result: competitor overview, saves 6–8 hours.

5. Exhibition Materials Preparation

Stand presentations, brochure texts, manager scripts — public materials.

Prompt Example
Prepare material package for [SPECIFY EXHIBITION]:

Parameters:
- Industry: [SPECIFY]
- Audience: [SPECIFY]
- Product: [SPECIFY]
- Exhibition goal: [SPECIFY: leads, brand, product]

Materials:
1. Stand slogan (3 variants, 5-7 words)
2. Brochure text (1 A4 page, structure: problem-solution-result)
3. Manager script (greeting → qualification → presentation → CTA)
4. Roll-up text (2 variants, 50 words)
5. Follow-up email for stand visitors

Tone: professional, no aggressive sales.

Result: material kit, saves 4–5 hours.

6. Marketing Materials Localization

Content adaptation for different markets — language, culture, regulations.

Prompt Example
Adapt marketing text for local market:

Original text (Russian):
[PASTE TEXT]

Target market: [SPECIFY COUNTRY]
Language: [SPECIFY LANGUAGE]

Adapt:
1. Translation with industry terminology
2. Cultural nuances (what works in this market)
3. Regulatory restrictions (what can't be promised)
4. Examples and cases (replace with local if possible)
5. CTA (call to action relevant to market)

Format: table "original → adaptation → comment"

Result: localized text, saves 3–4 hours.

7. Industry Trend Analysis

Summarization of public analytics, news, analyst reports.

Prompt Example (Web Search)
Analyze trends in [SPECIFY] industry for 2025–2026:

Sources (public):
- Industry media
- Analyst reports (McKinsey, BCG, Deloitte)
- Major player news
- LinkedIn publications from experts

Questions:
1. Key trends (top 5)
2. Technology changes
3. Regulatory shifts
4. Changing client behavior
5. New business models
6. Risks and opportunities for our company
7. Positioning recommendations

Format: analytical note (1-2 pages)

Result: trend overview, saves 5–6 hours.

8. Price Analytics from Exchange Quotes

Monitoring public exchange data for pricing policy.

Prompt Example (Web Search)
Conduct price analytics for commodity markets:

Indicators:
1. LME Aluminum (3-month futures) — 6-month dynamics
2. Baltic Dry Index (BDI) — freight trend
3. USD/RUB rate (if relevant)
4. Manufacturing sector PMI index

Questions:
- What is the raw material price dynamic?
- How does this affect [SPECIFY PRODUCT] cost?
- Forecast for next quarter (from analysts)
- Pricing policy recommendations (general, without specific figures)

Format: analytical bulletin

Result: market overview, saves 2–3 hours.

Knowledge Check

Question 1: Why is content plan development for external AI?

Because the end product (posts, articles) is public by definition
Because it doesn't contain commercial prices
Only if advertising budget is not specified

Question 2: Competitor analysis — which environment?

External AI — public data about products and prices
On-prem — commercial intelligence
Hybrid — data outside, conclusions inside

Question 3: Can you prepare investor pitch in external AI?

Yes, if it's general structure without financial data
Hybrid — structure and text external, final figures in on-prem
No, pitch always contains insider information
