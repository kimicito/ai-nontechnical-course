0.3 Cloud vs On-premise: GENAi for sensitive data

🎯 No code · ✅ Quiz at the end

🎯 Why does business need this?

60% of companies do not use AI due to fear of data leakage. on-premise accommodation.

⚠️ Real case: The purchasing manager has uploaded a list of suppliers with prices to ChatGPT.

📚 Cloud vs On-premise: in simple words

☁️ Cloud

What is this: You send data to the servers of another company (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google).

✅ Cheap, fast, no IT department needed

❌ Data goes beyond the company’s perimeter

🏢 On-premise (Own server)

What is this: The AI ​​model runs on a server within the company; the data does not leave the office.

✅ Full data control, confidentiality

❌ More expensive, you need a server and admin

Analogy for negotiations

💡 ANALOGY

Cloud = office rental in a business center. It’s convenient, but the concierge sees all your visitors, and the neighbors hear through the wall.

On-premise = your home. You are the owner, but you fix the faucets yourself and pay for the roof repairs.

🛠️ GENAi: golden mean

GENAi is a free interface (like ChatGPT) that works with models on your server.

When to choose GENAi:

  • Working with trade secrets (prices, suppliers, contracts)
  • Personal data of employees or clients
  • Financial statements before publication
  • Strategic plans and forecasts
  • Regulatory requirement to store data in the country (RF, PRC, EU)

Risk Matrix

🔴 HIGH

Contracts, prices, M&A

🟡 MEDIUM

Internal reports, HR

🟢 LOW

Marketing, public data

👨‍💻 How to assign a task to a developer

Template for IT Director: 
"We need to deploy a local LLM for the [NAME] department.

✅ Checklist for accepting the result

  • Doesn't the data leave the company's server?
  • Is there a backup?
  • Can users work without an IT specialist?
  • Response speed< 10 секунд? (проверить на пиковой нагрузке)
  • Is there a model update process?

🧰 Tools

  • GENAi (openwebui.com)— free interface, installed in 30 minutes
  • Ollama (ollama.com)— launching local models (Llama, Mistral, Qwen)
  • Qwen (Alibaba)— open-source model for commercial use
  • vLLM— acceleration of reasoning for on-premise (will require a developer)
