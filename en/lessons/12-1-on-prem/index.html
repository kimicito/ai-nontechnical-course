Level 13 — Logistics

13.1 Internal Logistics Tasks

⏱ 20 min · for logistics and supply chain specialists
Closed Loop (On-Prem)
All tasks in this lesson work with real volumes, routes, rates, and counterparties. Use an internal model or local LLM.

Why On-Prem Is Critical for Logistics

Logistics operates with sensitive data: commercial rates, contractual terms, shipping routes, and counterparty information. Sending this data to external services creates risks of leakage and unfair competition.

When on-prem is mandatory:
  • Scenario planning for supply chains (real volumes, routes, dependencies)
  • Freight quotations (commercial terms, negotiations)
  • Charter parties (legally significant terms)
  • Correspondence with brokers/shipowners on real rates
  • Railway transport requests with real volumes/directions
  • Route planning with regulatory restrictions
  • Correspondence with carriers on real terms
  • Risk assessment of a specific shipment delay
  • Efficiency reports (commercial data)
  • Claims work (legally significant)
  • Logistics outsourcing specs with real volumes
  • Capacity reservation plans for peak season

1. Supply Chain Scenario Planning

What if a port closes? A crossing is overloaded? Develop alternative scenarios.

Prompt Example (On-Prem)
Conduct scenario planning for a metal shipment to Europe for Q4 2026:

Base scenario: shipment from [SPECIFY PORT OF DEPARTURE] → [SPECIFY PORT OF DESTINATION], 45,000 t
Risks:
- [SPECIFY PORT]: possible restrictions (check stevedore contracts)
- [SPECIFY CROSSING]: enhanced inspections expected from 01.10.2026
- Alternative: [SPECIFY ALT. PORT] → [SPECIFY PORT OF DESTINATION] (surcharge $8/t)

Scenarios:
1. Base (probability 60%)
2. Shift to alternative port (probability 30%)
3. Rail route via [SPECIFY COUNTRY] → [SPECIFY COUNTRY] (probability 10%, $35/t more expensive)

For each: volume, timeline, cost, risks, switch trigger.

Result: scenario matrix for the board of directors, saves 3–4 hours.

3. Charter Party Preparation and Review

A charter party is a legally significant document. An LLM helps check for non-standard terms and articulate risks in text.

Prompt Example
Review the charter party for compliance with standard terms and identify risks:

Charter party text (excerpts):
- Vessel type: Capesize, dwt 180,000
- Cargo: [SPECIFY CARGO], 55,000 t (+/- 10%)
- Loading port: [SPECIFY PORT] (1 safe port)
- Discharge port: [SPECIFY PORT] (1 safe port)
- Freight rate: $18.5/t, FIOST
- Laytime: 5 days loading, 4 days discharge, Sundays/holidays excluded
- Demurrage: $18,000/day
- Term: "Owner not responsible for cargo shortfall if shore scales differ from draft survey by more than 0.5%"

Check:
1. Non-standard/risky terms (vs standard GENCON)
2. Missing clauses (standard for bulk carriers)
3. Conflicting wording
4. Recommendations for additions/amendments

Result: reviewed party with risks identified, saves 3–4 hours of lawyer time.

9. Logistics Efficiency Summary Report

Generating a text executive summary for management based on KPIs.

Prompt Example
Prepare a quarterly logistics report for Q2 2026 as text for the director:

Key metrics:
- Shipments: 127 voyages (plan: 120)
- OTIF (On Time In Full): 78% (plan: 85%, last year: 82%)
- Average shipping cost: $23.4/t (plan: $21, last year: $19.8)
- Port delays: average 3.2 days (norm: 2)
- Claims: 12 (last quarter: 7)

Provide:
1. Brief summary (3 sentences) — main problem
2. Trend analysis (what improved, what worsened vs last quarter and vs plan)
3. Text recommendations (3 specific actions)
4. Q3 risks (what could go wrong)

Tone: professional, no tables — text only.

Result: ready text report, saves 1.5 hours.

10. Claims Work for Delivery Failures

Generating legally sound claim text.

Prompt Example
Prepare a claim to the carrier:

Voyage: bulk carrier "[SPECIFY VESSEL NAME]", transporting [SPECIFY CARGO] [SPECIFY PORT] → [SPECIFY PORT]
Freight contract: No. [SPECIFY NUMBER] dated [SPECIFY DATE]

Breach:
- Actual arrival date: 2026-06-28 (planned: 2026-06-20)
- Delay: 8 days
- Reason stated by vessel: main engine breakdown
- Verification: breakdown occurred on 2026-06-18 (2 days after planned date), yet vessel did not reduce speed before the breakdown

Losses:
- Buyer contract penalty: $24,000
- Discharge equipment idle time: $8,000
- Additional warehousing costs: $3,200

Demands: compensation for losses $35,200 + contract penalty $8,000.

Result: legally sound claim, saves 2 hours.

Question 1: Why is route planning with regulatory restrictions on-prem?

Because it describes the company's cargo movement logic — a commercial risk
Because it contains carrier commercial rates
Only if the route passes through EU countries

Question 2: Can the Baltic Dry Index be monitored in the cloud?

Yes, it is a publicly available freight index
No, freight indices are commercially confidential
Only if it is historical data, not current

On-prem — everything contains trade secrets
Hybrid — anonymized template in the cloud, real volumes on-prem
External AI — specification structure is common across companies
