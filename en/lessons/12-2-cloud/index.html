13.2 External Logistics Tasks

⏱ 15 min · for logistics and supply chain professionals
Open Data (External AI)
All tasks in this lesson use public sources, common methodologies, open markets. You can use Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini.

Why Cloud Is Better Than On-Prem for Logistics

Cloud models win when you need:

  • Web search: monitor freight indices, railway tariffs, public restrictions
  • Methodology: develop a general strategy without real volumes
  • Benchmarking: compare industry practices of 3PL, KPIs
  • Forecasts: summarize public weather/geopolitical forecasts
  • Templates: create standard documents (CMR, requests)
Important: before sending to the cloud — check that your request contains no real volumes, routes, rates, or counterparty names. If it does — anonymize or send to on-prem.

1. Developing a General Logistics Strategy

Methodology for building a route network — without tying to a specific company.

Prompt Example (Claude with Web Search)
Prepare a structure for a logistics strategy for a metallurgical company:

Sections:
1. Current network analysis (typical stages: raw materials → production → customer)
2. Transport mode selection (sea/rail/road/maritime container) — criteria
3. Backup and redundancy (duplicate routes)
4. Inventory management (safety stock, reorder point)
5. 3PL vs in-house — selection criteria
6. IT systems (TMS, WMS, EDI)
7. KPIs (OTIF, cost per ton, lead time, carbon footprint)
8. Risk management (scenario planning, insurance)

Without specific volumes and routes.

Result: ready-made strategy, saves 5–6 hours.

2. Monitoring Freight Indices

Baltic Dry Index and analogs — public data for benchmarking.

Prompt Example (Web Search)
Monitor freight indices (July 2026):

Indices:
1. Baltic Dry Index (BDI) — 3-month dynamics
2. Baltic Capesize Index (BCI)
3. Baltic Panamax Index (BPI)
4. China Containerized Freight Index (CCFI)

Questions:
- What is the dynamics since the beginning of the year?
- What factors are at play (weather, geopolitics, seasonality)?
- Forecast for Q3–Q4 2026 (according to analysts)
- Which routes are rising in price the fastest?
- Recommendations for hedging freight risks

Output a table with sources.

Result: freight market overview, saves 2–3 hours.

5. Collecting and Summarizing Weather/Catastrophe Forecasts

Generative function — summarizing public reports, not forecasting per se.

Prompt Example
Summarize open forecasts affecting delivery routes in August 2026:

Regions:
- South China Sea (typhoons)
- Cape of Good Hope (storms)
- Suez Canal (weather, geopolitics)
- Baltic Sea (autumn storms)

For each:
1. Public forecasts (meteorological services, NOAA)
2. Risk period (dates)
3. Probability and intensity
4. Impact on shipping (port closures, restrictions)
5. Recommendations (avoid, bypass, reserve)

Format: alert for the logistics department.

Result: risk summary, saves 1.5 hours.

6. Benchmarking Logistics Costs and Practices

Comparison with industry standards — public data.

Prompt Example (Web Search)
Benchmark logistics costs in metallurgy (2024–2026):

Questions:
1. What percentage of revenue do logistics costs represent for large metallurgical companies?
2. What KPIs do industry leaders use (OTIF, cost per ton, carbon intensity)?
3. Which 3PL providers work with metallurgy (FESCO, TransContainer, DP World)?
4. Which TMS IT systems are implemented (SAP TM, 1C Logistics, Ortec)?
5. What trends: green logistics, digital twin, blockchain in document flow?

Prepare a structured report with sources.

Result: industry practices overview, saves 4–5 hours.

8. Preparing Alerts for Detours/Shipment Delays

General routing scenario — not tied to a specific cargo.

Prompt Example
Prepare an alert methodology and recommendations for logistics disruptions:

Typical scenarios:
1. Storm at loading port — delay of 3–5 days
2. Border crossing overload — queue of 10+ days
3. Strike at unloading port — indefinite delay
4. Accident on railway tracks — bypass via alternative route
5. Regulatory restrictions — sudden ban on port entry

For each scenario:
- Alert trigger (what we track)
- Who is notified (logistics, procurement, customer)
- Action options (bypass, postpone, replace)
- Message template for the counterparty
- Decision deadline

Methodology without specific cargo and dates.

Result: ready-made methodology, saves 3–4 hours.
