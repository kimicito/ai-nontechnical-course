6.2 · AI hallucinations: when a machine lies convincingly

🎯 No code · ✅ Interactive quiz

🎯 Why does business need this?

AI doesn't know the difference between fact and fiction.

Real case: A lawyer used AI to prepare a court case.

Danger: Hallucinations are especially dangerous in business because the AI ​​sounds confident and professional. Always.

📚 What we are studying

What are hallucinations

Hallucination- when AI generates information that looks plausible, but is false or unverifiable.

Types of hallucinations:

  • Facts:"Company X was founded in 1995" (actually 2005)
  • Sources:"According to an article in Forbes..." (article does not exist)
  • Quotes:"As Elon Musk said..." (Musk didn't say that)
  • Statistics:"The market grew by 150%..." (the figure is fictitious)

Why AI hallucinates

CauseExplanation
No dataAI doesn't know the answer, but generates "most likely"
Outdated dataTraining ended on a certain date
ConfusionMixed facts from different sources
Wants to pleaseAI strives to give a “complete” answer, adds details

How to recognize a hallucination

  • ✅ Too specific details without sources
  • ✅ The answer sounds “ideal” - without nuances
  • ✅ AI says something that contradicts your knowledge
  • ✅ Numbers are “round” or too precise
  • ✅ No links to sources (for facts)

🛠️ How to apply

Methods to combat hallucinations

1. Ask for sources

Prompt
"Give an answer with sources. If you are not sure, say 'I don't know.'

2. RAG (search your documents)

AI only responds based on your documents, not general knowledge.

3. Checking critical facts

Numbers, dates, names, laws - always check manually.

4. Use specialized models

Models trained on legal/medical data hallucinate less frequently in their domain.

✅ Checklist for accepting the result

  • Have all the facts been verified?
  • Are there sources for each statement?
  • AI said “I don’t know” where it’s not sure?
  • Have extreme values ​​(edge ​​cases) been tested?
  • Is there a review process for critical decisions?
  • Do users know that AI can make mistakes?
