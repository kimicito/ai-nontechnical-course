Level 14 — Sales & Marketing

14.1 Internal Tasks: On-Prem

⏱ 25 min · for sales managers, commercial directors, marketers
Closed Loop (On-Prem)
All tasks in this lesson work with real prices, deal terms, client data, and commercial forecasts. Use an internal model or local LLM.

Why On-Prem is Critical for Sales

Sales is a mirror function of procurement: where you are the buyer with your negotiating position, here you are the seller with symmetrically sensitive data. Price, discount, payment terms — all are trade secrets regardless of which side of the deal you're on.

Moreover, demand forecasting and sales planning directly flow into production planning, making this task more sensitive than it appears at first glance.

When On-Prem is Mandatory:
  • Pricing and calculation of individual discounts (foundation of negotiating position)
  • Preparation and review of sales contracts (real deal terms)
  • Client base analysis — segmentation, potential, churn (client dependency)
  • Demand forecasting and sales planning (internal operational data)
  • Preparation for negotiations with key clients (negotiation strategy)
  • CRM notes and summarization of client calls (personal data + commercial information)
  • Responses to RFQ/RFP from clients (real prices before submission)
  • Win/loss analysis for specific deals (reveals reasons for losing clients)
  • Sales plan execution reports (management financial information)
  • Campaign effectiveness analysis on real internal metrics (budgets, ROI)
  • Investor/analyst pitches before official publication (insider information)

1. Pricing and Individual Terms Calculation

Price calculation for a specific client considering volume, discounts, payment terms, and logistics — a trade secret.

Prompt Example (On-Prem)
Prepare a commercial proposal for client [SPECIFY NAME]:

Input Data:
- Product: [SPECIFY PRODUCT]
- Volume: [SPECIFY VOLUME] units/month
- Base price: [SPECIFY PRICE] $/unit
- Deal history: [SPECIFY DISCOUNT HISTORY]
- Competitors: offer [SPECIFY COMPETITOR PRICES]
- Payment terms: [SPECIFY TERMS]
- Logistics: delivery to [SPECIFY ADDRESS]

Calculate:
1. Price with volume discount
2. Options: prepayment vs deferred payment (price impact)
3. Comparison with competitors (positioning)
4. Minimum price (lower profitability boundary)
5. Negotiation strategy recommendation

Result: justified price with lower boundary, saves 2–3 hours.

4. Preparation for Negotiations with Key Clients

Negotiation strategy, BATNA, leverage — as in procurement, just on the other side of the table.

Prompt Example

Context:
- Current contract: [SPECIFY TERMS], expires [SPECIFY DATE]
- Volume: [SPECIFY] units/year
- Client complaints: [SPECIFY CLAIMS]
- Competitors: [SPECIFY WHO] offers [SPECIFY TERMS]
- Our position: [SPECIFY GOALS]

Develop:
1. BATNA (our best alternative)
2. ZOPA (zone of possible agreement)
3. Leverage (what we can offer/what we risk)

Result: ready strategy, saves 3 hours.

Knowledge Check

Question 1: Why is demand forecasting on-prem and not external AI?

Because it directly flows into production planning and contains operational data
Because it contains product pricing
Only if forecast includes historical data over 3 years

Question 2: Can win/loss analysis be done in external AI?

Yes, if client names are anonymized
No, it reveals reasons for losing clients to competitors — critically sensitive
Only for lost deals with major clients

Question 3: Which environment for CRM notes after a client meeting?

External AI — it's just text summarization
On-prem — personal contacts data + commercial information
Hybrid — anonymize and send to cloud
