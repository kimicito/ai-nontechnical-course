10.1 Reality: What AI Can Do in PR and Communications Today

20 min · for PR directors, GR, communications professionals · boundaries matter most

Main thesis: AI doesn't replace the PR director, but it accelerates routine work 10×. Reputation is an intangible asset that can be destroyed by a single draft. That's why in PR, the "internal / external" boundary is more critical than in procurement.

In procurement, a leak means prices and terms. In PR, a leak means the company's position before approval — which becomes a second crisis on top of the first.

Why PR Is Harder Than Procurement

The cost of a PR mistake is international headlines:
  • Unverified press release → reputational crisis within hours
  • Leaked internal position → opponents use it against you
  • Wrong tone in a crisis → audience perceives it as mockery
  • Error in ESG report → sanctions and exclusion from indices
  • Inappropriate joke in a GR speech → license revocation or protocol
Reputation = intangible asset:
  • Brand value of a major company — billions of dollars
  • Reputation recovery takes years; loss takes hours
  • ESG rating affects cost of capital and market access
  • GR position determines licenses, quotas, government contracts

Three Baskets of PR Tasks

Everything PR does falls into three baskets by AI implementation speed:

🟢 Basket 1: Quick Wins (Tomorrow)

  • Press release drafts — structure, facts, quotes. AI writes the first version, human edits.
  • Media and social media monitoring — auto-collection of mentions, sentiment, topic clustering. AI analyzes streams.
  • Translations for international media — releases in English, Chinese, Arabic. Public content can be prepared externally.
  • Speaker preparation — Q&A, journalist question forecasting, training sessions.
  • ESG reports: form — structure, alignment with GRI/ESRS, completeness check of disclosures.
  • Competitor analysis — what they write about them, what topics they raise, how they respond to crises.

🟡 Basket 2: Needs Development (Month–Quarter)

  • Crisis bot — response scenarios, escalation, team coordination. Requires tuning on real cases.
  • GR analytics — regulatory monitoring, change forecasting, business impact assessment. Needs integration with law databases.
  • Auto-generated social media content — carousels, posts, stories with brand book compliance. Requires visual approval.
  • Internal PR knowledge base — all past releases, responses, positions. Semantic search, not keyword search.
  • ESG reports: content — data collection from 15 departments, metric validation, carbon footprint calculation.

🔴 Basket 3: Needs Agents (Half a Year–Year)

  • Autonomous crisis manager — AI tracks, assesses, proposes strategy, coordinates with lawyers, and issues response. Requires access to internal data and authority.
  • Predictive reputation analytics — predicting crises before they appear in media. Model on historical data + social networks.
  • Automated lobbying — generating positions on bills, sending to authorities, tracking response. Ethically and legally complex.
  • Personalized communications — different messages for different audiences in real-time. Requires segment data.

The Internal / External Boundary: More Critical Than in Procurement

In PR, this boundary isn't just important — it determines company survival in a crisis.

🔒 Internal — never leaves the perimeter:
  • Company position before approval (drafts, internal memos)
  • Crisis scenarios and response plans
  • GR strategy and negotiating positions with regulators
  • ESG data before audit (may contain insider information)
  • Competitor weakness analysis (legally risky)
  • Personal data of journalists and influencers
🌍 External — can be prepared outside:
  • Approved press releases and statements
  • Public ESG reports (after audit)
  • Translations of public materials
  • Analysis of public media and social networks
  • Speech drafts (without internal position)
  • General market and industry trends
Boundary Example
Never in the cloud:
"Our position on the plant incident: we partially admit fault,
are ready to compensate, but ask not to blow it up.
BATNA: if pressure continues — we'll close the topic
through sponsorship of a local foundation."

OK in the cloud:
"Write a press release about the launch of a new production facility.
Facts: commissioned on July 15, capacity 100 t/year,
200 new jobs, investment 5 billion rubles."

Where AI Already Works in PR: Prompt Examples

1. Press Release Draft

Prompt Example (External)
Write a press release about the launch of a new alumina production shop. Facts:
- Launch: July 15, 2026
- Capacity: 100 thousand tons per year
- Investment: 5 billion rubles
- 200 new jobs
- Technology: proprietary development, 15 patents
- Ecology: emissions reduced by 30% vs analogs

Tone: professional, factual. Length: 400 words.
Target audience: business media, investors.

2. Media Sentiment Monitoring

Prompt Example (External — Public Data)
Here are 50 mentions of the company this week (file mentions.xlsx).

Analyze:
1. Distribution by sentiment (positive / neutral / negative)
2. Top 5 topics
3. Top 5 media by reach
4. Key quotes (one per topic)
5. Recommendations: which topics to respond to, what to ignore

3. Speaker Preparation (Q&A)

Prompt Example (Internal — On-Prem)
Preparing the CEO for a year-end press conference.

Internal data (not for publication):
- Revenue: +12%, but EBITDA down 3%
- Reason: rising logistics costs
- Risk of leaving the EU market in Q4
- Negotiations with Asian partners — at NDA stage

Generate:
1. 20 questions journalists will definitely ask
2. Recommended answers (what to say / what not to say)
3. Trigger questions: what not to answer under any circumstances
4. Key messages (3 theses to repeat 3 times)

4. Crisis Communication: First Response

Prompt Example (Internal — On-Prem)
Situation: chemical spill at a plant.
No casualties, no threat to the population.
Local media is already writing.

Internal position (not for publication):
- Cause: human factor, procedure violation
- Investigation: internal, results in 2 weeks
- Compensation: discussing with district administration

Write:
1. First operational comment (2–3 sentences)
2. Draft official statement (5–7 sentences)
3. Internal memo for employees: what to say, what not to say
4. Action checklist for the next 24 hours

5. ESG Report: Completeness Check

Prompt Example (Internal — On-Prem)
Here is a draft ESG report (file ESG_draft.pdf).

Check compliance with GRI and ESRS standards:
1. Which mandatory disclosures are missing?
2. Which metrics don't match the methodology?
3. Where is additional data needed?
4. What are the "greenwashing" risks?

Provide a table: standard item → status → recommendation.

Practical Takeaway for Sanctioned Companies

Companies like RUSAL, En+, Norilsk Nickel live in a special mode: any word can become a reason for new sanctions, exclusion from indices, or license revocation.

PR Specifics in Sanctions Mode:
  • Every statement is political. A press release about production is read as a position on sanctions.
  • ESG is a survival tool. Good ESG rating = access to capital in Asia and the Middle East.
  • GR and PR have merged. Any public communication affects regulatory decisions.
  • Cascading crises. Sanctions → logistics problems → incidents → media → new sanctions. The chain must be broken.
Where AI Helps These Specific Companies:
  • 24/7 Monitoring — tracking mentions in 50+ countries, auto-translation, sentiment. A human can't handle it.
  • Predictive analytics — which topics are gaining momentum, which media are synchronizing (sign of an information campaign).
  • Fast localization — releases in 10 languages in hours, not weeks. Critical for Asian and Middle Eastern markets.
  • Position alignment — AI checks compliance with internal position, flags risks before publication.
  • ESG compliance — auto-checking reports against international standards, finding issues before audit.
What Cannot Be Automated in Sanctions Mode:
  • Strategic communication decisions (AI doesn't bear responsibility)
  • Direct contacts with regulators and lobbyists (requires human trust)
  • Key negotiations with investors (NDA, insider information)
  • Final editing of crisis statements (tone is strategy)

Summary: Rules for a PR Director Working with AI

  1. AI is an assistant, not a replacement. The final publication decision is always human. Tone, context, politics — these aren't about algorithms.
  2. The boundary is sacred. Drafts, positions, crisis plans — on-prem. Public facts — can be in the cloud.
  3. Speed ≠ quality. AI writes fast but can err in facts, tone, context. Everything is checked by a human.
  4. ESG is the new front. Automating ESG reporting and compliance is a competitive advantage, especially under sanctions.
  5. GR and PR are one. Any communication affects regulatory risks. AI helps track the connection.
