15.2 External Tasks: External AI

⏱ 20 min · for lawyers, compliance specialists
Open Data (External AI)
All tasks in this lesson — standard templates, public legislation, open court decisions, training materials. You can use Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini.

What Can Be Done in External AI

Unlike tasks with specific cases and counterparties, working with open legal field is safe and effective:

  • Drafting standard contracts from templates (without real terms)
  • Legislation change monitoring (public sources)
  • Case law analysis (open decisions)
  • Patent search (open databases)
  • Training materials (general topics)
  • Legal practice benchmarking (public data)
Main Rule: if the request doesn't contain company names, amounts, specific dates, personal data, strategic plans — you can use external AI.

1. Standard Contract Templates

Drafting contract structure without real terms — public methodology.

Prompt Example (External AI)
Prepare a standard supply contract:

Parameters (without specific terms):
- Type: goods supply contract
- Legal system: [SPECIFY COUNTRY]
- Buyer side: large company (B2B)
- Supplier side: manufacturer
- Subject: [SPECIFY GOODS TYPE without quantity/price]

Draft:
1. Preamble (standard)
2. Subject of contract (description structure)
3. Price and payment procedure (standard formulations)
4. Delivery and risk transfer (Incoterms — standard)
5. Quality and acceptance (standard terms)
6. Liability of parties (without specific penalties)
7. Force majeure (standard article)
8. Dispute resolution (standard options)
9. Final provisions

Format: for each article — base text + alternatives

Result: standard template for legal department, saves 3–4 hours.

2. Legislation Change Monitoring

Summarizing legislation changes on specific topics.

Prompt Example (Web Search)
Analyze legislation changes for the last 6 months:

Topics:
1. [SPECIFY AREA: corporate law/tax/labor/antitrust]
2. [SPECIFY SECOND TOPIC]
3. [SPECIFY THIRD TOPIC]

Jurisdiction: [SPECIFY COUNTRY/REGION]

Provide:
1. List of key changes (date, document number, essence)
2. Business impact analysis (general, without company-specific reference)
3. Effective dates
4. Required company actions in general
5. Preparation recommendations

Format: analytical note (1-2 pages)

Result: changes overview, saves 4–5 hours.

3. Case Law Analysis

Search and analysis of open court decisions on similar issues.

Prompt Example (Web Search)
Analyze case law on issue:

Issue: [SPECIFY LEGAL ISSUE without specific case reference]
Jurisdiction: [SPECIFY]
Period: last 2 years

Provide:
1. Selection of relevant cases (5-10 decisions)
2. Brief summary of each case
3. Court position (key conclusions)
4. Practice trends (how court approach changes)
5. Risks for companies in general
6. Risk minimization recommendations

Important: only public decisions, no company mentions unless public case.

Result: practice overview, saves 5–6 hours.

4. Patent Search and Freedom-to-Operate Analysis

Search in open patent databases, freedom-to-operate analysis.

Prompt Example (Web Search)
Conduct patent search on technology:

Technology: [SPECIFY AREA without specific product details]
Keywords: [SPECIFY 3-5 TERMS]
IPC classes: [SPECIFY if known]
Jurisdictions: [SPECIFY: US, EP, CN, etc.]

Provide:
1. Search for relevant patents (5-10)
2. Analysis of each patent (what is protected, term, status)
3. Freedom-to-operate analysis (any obstacles)
4. Workaround recommendations (if any)
5. Next steps recommendation

Important: this is preliminary analysis, not a substitute for patent attorney.

Result: preliminary analysis, saves 3–4 hours (but doesn't replace patent attorney).

5. Employee Training Materials

Preparing training materials on general legal topics.

Prompt Example
Prepare training material for employees:

Topic: [SPECIFY: trade secrets/anti-corruption/competition/personal data]
Audience: [SPECIFY: all employees/managers/sales]
Format: presentation (10 slides) + test (5 questions)

Draft:
1. Training objectives (what employee should know)
2. Key provisions (simple language)
3. Situation examples (hypothetical, not real)
4. Behavior rules (do's and don'ts)
5. Liability for violation
6. Test (5 multiple-choice questions)
7. Self-check checklist

Tone: accessible, no legal jargon.

Result: ready training material, saves 3–4 hours.

6. Industry Legal Practice Benchmarking

Comparing legal processes with similar companies based on public data.

Prompt Example (Web Search)
Conduct legal practice benchmarking in industry:

Industry: [SPECIFY]
Comparison parameters:
- Legal department structure
- Legal tech usage
- External lawyers vs in-house
- Litigation load (typical disputes)
- Legal services cost (public data)
- Compliance practices

Sources: industry reports, publications, conferences

Provide:
1. Overview of 3-5 industry companies (public data)
2. Comparison table
3. Best practices
4. Legal function optimization recommendations

Important: only public data, no specific contract amounts.

Result: industry practices overview, saves 4–5 hours.

Knowledge Check

Question 1: Can a supply contract with specific price be drafted in external AI?

Yes, if it's a standard contract
No, specific price is trade secret and goes to on-prem
Yes, but only for small companies

Question 2: Case law analysis — which environment?

External AI — court decisions are public
On-prem — case law is confidential
Hybrid — anonymize company names

Question 3: Patent search — which environment?

External AI — patent databases are open
On-prem — this is company intellectual property
Hybrid — databases outside, analysis inside
