13.3 Hybrid Logistics Tasks

⏱ 20 min · for logistics professionals
Hybrid Approach
The general structure and methodology is prepared in the external AI.
Real data (cargo, dates, rates, routes) is added on-prem.

The Principle of the Hybrid Approach for Logistics

Key rule: the more sensitive the data (specific counterparties, financial indicators) — the stricter the on-prem. General methodology (document structure, analysis categories) can safely go to the external perimeter.

1. Delay Risk Assessment (Hybrid)

Cloud: general logic — "if there's a storm at port X, how does it typically affect bulk cargo".
On-prem: linking to a specific vessel, cargo, client.

Step 1: Cloud — Assessment Methodology
Prepare a methodology for assessing delay risk in maritime delivery:

Factors:
1. Weather along the route (storms, typhoons)
2. Port congestion (queues, stevedores)
3. Geopolitics (restrictions, bans)
4. Vessel technical condition (age, class)
5. Seasonality (peak periods)

Risk scale: low (delay ≤1 day) / medium (2–3 days) / high (≥4 days)

Impact calculation method: delay cost = penalties + demurrage + alternative expenses

2. Detour/Rerouting Alerts (Hybrid)

Cloud: general routing scenario externally.
On-prem: final decision with real dates and cargo.

Step 1: Cloud — Response Scenarios
Prepare response scenarios for delay at the loading port:

Scenario: loading delay of 5 days (storm, equipment breakdown)

Options:
A. Wait (keep vessel in port) — risk: demurrage
B. Redirect to alternative port — risk: +distance, +bunker
C. Switch to a more available vessel — risk: overload, damage
D. Postpone shipment to next window — risk: buyer penalty

Selection criteria: cost, timeline, cargo damage risk, reputation.
