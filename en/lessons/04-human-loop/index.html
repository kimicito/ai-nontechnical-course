4.4 · Human in the loop: when should AI ask permission

🎯 No code · ✅ Interactive quiz

🎯 Why does business need this?

The AI ​​is wrong. are expensive: incorrect price in the contract, incorrect purchasing decision, leak of confidential data.

Real case: The AI ​​agent automatically approved the purchase at an inflated price.

Solution:“Human-in-the-loop” - AI performs the task, but at critical points it stops and waits for human confirmation.

📚 What we are studying

3 levels of control

🟢 Full autonomy

AI acts without confirmation

For: routine tasks, low risk

Example: Daily email summary

🟡 Partial control

AI suggests, human confirms

For: important decisions, medium risk

Example: approval of purchases > 100,000 ₽

🔴 Full control

AI only cooks, humans decide

For: critical decisions, high risk

Example: signing a contract, dismissal

🛠️ How to apply

Scenario: procurement automation

With human control
AI analyzes the supplier’s CP:
< 50 000 ₽ → Automatically approves
If amount 50,000 - 500,000 ₽ → Sends to procurement for confirmation
If amount >500,000 ₽ → Sends to the director for confirmation

Result: Routine is automated, important decisions are under control.

How to set up a "man in the loop"

  1. Define thresholds: Under what conditions is confirmation required?
  2. Select notification method: Email, Telegram, corporate messenger, task in CRM
  3. Set response time: How long should I wait for confirmation?
  4. Set up escalation: If the first person did not respond, who should I redirect to?

👨‍💻 How to delegate to a developer

Task template

"We need a 'man in the loop' mechanism for the AI agent [name].

✅ Checklist for accepting the result

  • Do all critical operations require confirmation?
  • Do notifications arrive on time?
  • Are there clear thresholds for each level of control?
  • Does escalation work?
  • Are timeouts set wisely?
  • Is there a log of all decisions?
