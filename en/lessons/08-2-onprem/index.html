8.2 Tasks Inside GENAi: On-Prem

15 min · for civil engineers · closed-loop

This lesson covers tasks that a construction company solves internally, with data that cannot be sent to the internet. Estimates, certificates, correspondence with subcontractors, claims — all of this contains trade secrets and confidential information.

Solution: on-prem LLM (e.g., Qwen, GigaChat, YandexGPT Enterprise) within the company's closed perimeter. Data never leaves the perimeter, but you get all the power of generative AI.

Why On-Prem for Construction

What cannot be sent to the cloud:
  • Estimates, KS-2, KS-3 with prices and volumes
  • Claims and claim correspondence with subcontractors
  • As-built documentation and concealed works
  • Meeting minutes with commercial terms
  • Design briefs with budget constraints
What can be done on-prem:
  • Draft a claim with argumentation — in 10 minutes
  • Check as-built documentation for completeness
  • Compose meeting minutes from audio recording or notes
  • Verify KS-2/KS-3 against actual volumes
  • Write a design brief and construction supervision terms

1. Claims — Drafting Argumentation

A PTO engineer or site supervisor prepares a claim to a subcontractor for missed deadlines, poor quality work, or material shortages. Previously: hours of document review + legal consultation. Now: the LLM prepares a draft, the lawyer validates it.

Prompt Example
I am preparing a claim to the subcontractor LLC "StroyMontazh".

Input data:
- Contract No. 45 dated 15.02.2026, completion deadline: 30.06.2026
- Actual completion: 15.08.2026 (46 days delay)
- Causes of delay per my version: delayed rebar delivery by subcontractor (10 days), worker shortage (remaining 36 days)
- Penalty per contract: 0.1% of contract price for each day of delay
- Contract price: 15,000,000 ₽
- I sent a risk notification of deadline breach on 15.05.2026

Prepare:
1. Claim argumentation with references to standard contract clauses
2. Penalty calculation
3. Demands: rectify defects + pay the penalty
4. Response deadline: 10 business days
5. References to Federal Law 44 or Civil Code of the Russian Federation (as applicable)

Format: ready draft letter for lawyer validation.
Result: a structured claim draft with penalty calculation, references to legislation and contract. The engineer saves 2–3 hours, the lawyer spends 15 minutes on review instead of 2 hours writing from scratch.

2. Checking As-Built Documentation for Completeness

Before handing over the facility to the client or before the certificate of completed works, the engineer checks whether all as-built documentation is collected. OKS sheets, concealed works certificates, material certificates, logs — dozens of items.

Prompt Example
Check the completeness of as-built documentation for the facility "Shopping Center, Building A".

Here is the list of documents I have collected (file OKS_list.xlsx):
- As-built drawings (sheets 1–45)
- Concealed works certificates (sheets 1–120)
- Material certificates (folder /certs/)
- Works log (log_2026.docx)
- Incoming control log
- As-built geodetic log
- Test protocols

Completeness requirements:
- Each type of work must have a concealed works certificate
- Each material must have a certificate valid on the date of use
- Geodetic survey must be present at all stages

Check:
1. Which documents are missing compared to requirements
2. Which certificates are expired
3. Which concealed works certificates lack corresponding drawings
4. List of missing items with priority
Result: a list of missing documents with priorities. The PTO engineer saves 3–4 hours on manual folder review.

3. Correspondence with Subcontractors, Meeting Minutes

After a site meeting, the supervisor or engineer must prepare minutes. Previously: recall what was said, re-listen to audio, write manually. With on-prem LLM: upload the audio recording or notes — get ready minutes.

Prompt Example
Prepare minutes of a working meeting at the construction site.

Input data:
Date: 14.07.2026
Location: construction site, supervisor's office
Attendees:
- Ivanov A.P. (client representative)
- Petrov S.V. (chief project engineer, CPE)
- Sidorov K.M. (site supervisor, subcontractor LLC "StroyMontazh")
- Kuznetsova L.V. (PTO engineer, general contractor)

Discussion topics (notes):
1. Delay in monolithic works by 12 days. Cause: delayed formwork delivery. Decision: engage a second crew, work in 2 shifts starting 20.07.
2. Claim on plaster quality on the 3rd floor. Partial dismantling and rework required. Deadline: by 25.07.
3. Approved replacement of tile supplier from "Kerama" to "GranitStroy" — price 8% lower, delivery in 14 days.
4. Next meeting: 28.07.2026.

Prepare:
- Standard minutes with decisions recorded
- Action items with responsible persons and deadlines
- Separate: list of risks to be escalated to management
Result: ready minutes with action items, deadlines, and responsible persons. The supervisor saves 1–1.5 hours on preparation.

4. Analyzing KS-2/KS-3 Against Actual Data

The subcontractor submits KS-2 (certificate of completed works) and KS-3 (estimate). The engineer must check whether the volumes in the certificates match the actual ones. Previously: manually cross-check against logs, plans, photos. With on-prem: upload documents and ask.

Prompt Example
Check whether KS-2 and KS-3 match actual work volumes.

Input data:
- KS-2 for June 2026 (file KS2_june.pdf)
- KS-3 for June 2026 (file KS3_june.pdf)
- Works accounting log (file log_june.xlsx)
- Site photo report (folder /photos/june/)

Check:
1. Concrete volumes per KS-2 vs. actual per log (difference in m³)
2. Rebar volumes: accepted per KS-3 vs. actually installed
3. Presence of works not confirmed by photo report
4. Consistency of unit prices in KS-3 with contract estimate
5. Overtime works: whether confirmed by order

Output:
- List of discrepancies with amounts
- Recommendations: what to accept, what to reject
- Which documents to request from the subcontractor to resolve
Result: a discrepancy table with amounts and recommendations. The PTO engineer saves 4–6 hours on cross-checking.

5. Design Brief, Construction Supervision Brief

The client prepares a technical specification for the designer or for the organization that will perform construction supervision. Previously: search for templates, adapt, check for completeness. With on-prem LLM: describe the task — get a structured brief.

Prompt Example — Design Brief
Prepare a technical specification for the design of a shopping center.

Input data:
- Land area: 1.2 ha
- Building footprint: 8,500 m²
- Number of floors: 2 floors + basement
- Purpose: retail spaces, warehouse, parking
- Climate zone: III-B (central Russia)
- Seismicity: 6 points
- Required energy efficiency class: B
- Design budget: up to 5,000,000 ₽
- Timeline: 4 months
- Stages: sketch design → DD → WD

The brief must include:
1. General provisions
2. Input data (urban development plan, geological surveys)
3. Design documentation composition per list
4. Requirements for structural solutions
5. Requirements for engineering systems
6. Requirements for energy efficiency
7. Requirements for landscaping and greening
8. Timelines and stages
9. Approval and acceptance procedure
10. Restrictions and prohibitions
Prompt Example — Construction Supervision Brief
Prepare a technical specification for construction supervision.

Input data:
- Facility: shopping center, Building A, 8,500 m²
- Developer: LLC "DevelopProject"
- General contractor: LLC "StroyGrand"
- Construction period: 18 months
- Structural fire hazard class: C0
- Responsibility level: elevated

The brief must include:
1. Scope and content of construction supervision
2. List of controlled parameters (geometry, materials, technologies)
3. Inspection frequency by stage
4. Reporting requirements
5. Procedure for documenting violations and issuing orders
6. Interaction with client, general contractor, supervisory authorities
7. Requirements for qualifications of supervisory organization personnel
8. Supervision timelines and stages
9. Parties' responsibilities
Result: a structured brief per GOST and standard requirements. The engineer saves 4–6 hours on preparing the document from scratch.

Knowledge Check

Question 1: Why can't claims and KS-2/KS-3 be sent to cloud ChatGPT?

Because ChatGPT cannot read PDFs
Because this is trade secret and confidential data
Because construction documents are too large

Question 2: What role does the lawyer play when preparing a claim via on-prem LLM?

The lawyer writes the claim from scratch, the LLM only formats it
The LLM prepares a draft, the lawyer validates and refines it
The lawyer does not participate, the LLM does everything alone

Question 3: What is checked when analyzing KS-2/KS-3 via LLM?

Consistency of volumes in certificates against actual data and photo report
Spelling and document formatting
Presence of signatures from all parties

Question 4: What input format is needed to generate meeting minutes?

Only audio recording of the meeting
Audio recording or text notes with key decisions
Only video recording from surveillance cameras
