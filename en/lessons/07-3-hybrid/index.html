7.3 Hybrid Tasks: Data Anonymization

15 min · for procurement specialists · boundary of contours

Some tasks are on the boundary: they can be done in the cloud, but only if the data is anonymized. Replace the supplier name with "Supplier A," remove specific prices — and the task becomes safe for an external model.

In this lesson: how to anonymize data, which tasks are allowed in hybrid mode, and how to prepare a request for developers for automation.

Rule: Anonymize Before Cloud

What MUST NOT be sent to the cloud (even partially):
  • Specific prices from quotations
  • Supplier names (unless they are giants like Siemens)
  • Purchase volumes for specific items
  • Contract terms (penalties, discounts, payment deferrals)
  • Negotiation position, BATNA, supplier weaknesses
What CAN be sent after anonymization:
  • Comparison of quotation structure (without prices) — "Supplier A vs Supplier B"
  • Analysis of completeness of response to TOR (without names)
  • Role-play negotiation rehearsal (anonymous scenario)
  • Preparation of general arguments (without specific figures)

Anonymization Checklist (5 Steps)

  1. Replace names: "LLC PromTechSnab" → "Supplier A"
  2. Replace prices with relative ones: "1,200,000 ₽" → "base price +20%" or "Price X"
  3. Remove volumes: "50 units" → "medium-sized batch"
  4. Remove contract dates: "until 31.12.2025" → "contract until end of year"
  5. Check: if you send the anonymized text to a colleague — will they understand what it's about, but not know the supplier? If yes — it can go to the cloud.

1. Role-Play Negotiation Rehearsal

A procurement specialist is preparing for complex negotiations. They need to rehearse a scenario. Data can be anonymized and a cloud model can be used as the "opponent."

Example prompt (anonymized)
Conduct a role-play negotiation rehearsal with me.

Scenario (anonymized):
- I am a procurement specialist at an industrial company
- I need to reduce the price for equipment supply
- I have 2 alternative suppliers with prices 15% lower
- The current supplier (Supplier A) delayed delivery last time
- I need a 3-week lead time, their standard is 6 weeks
- I am willing to discuss volume, but not disclose specific figures

You play the role of Supplier A. Your goal is to maintain price and lead times.
My goal is to reduce price and accelerate lead time.

Start negotiations. Respond like a real supplier — 
with objections, counterarguments, attempts to deflect.
Result: 15–20 minutes of rehearsal with a "live" opponent. The procurement specialist practices arguments, learns to deflect objections, and prepares for the real conversation.

2. Comparison of Quotation Structure (Without Prices)

You need to understand which supplier responded more completely to the TOR, not who is cheaper. Prices are removed, only structure and completeness are compared.

Example prompt (anonymized)
Compare 3 commercial quotations by structure and completeness of response.

Data anonymized:
- Supplier A, Supplier B, Supplier C
- Prices removed, only relative: "below market", "market", "above market"

Compare by criteria:
1. Completeness of response to TOR (are all points covered)
2. Presence of technical descriptions (yes/no, quality)
3. Presence of certificates (list)
4. Warranty terms (duration, scope)
5. Additional services (installation, training, service)

Do not name specific prices. Give a recommendation on completeness.

3. Preparation of General Arguments

A procurement specialist needs a list of arguments for negotiations in general terms — not tied to a specific deal. A cloud model generates the base, then on-prem adds specifics.

Example prompt (anonymized)
I am preparing for negotiations on the procurement of industrial equipment.

Context (anonymized):
- Market: 3–4 large suppliers, high competition
- My position: large customer, regular purchases
- Problem: supplier wants to increase price and lead times

Prepare general arguments for negotiations:
1. Arguments for price reduction (market, volumes, competition)
2. Arguments for accelerating lead times (planning, reserves, penalties)
3. Counterarguments to typical supplier objections
4. Polite formulations for a firm position

Without specific prices, names, or volumes. 
Only templates that I will then adapt.

Request to Developers for Anonymization Automation

If procurement specialists often anonymize documents manually — this can be automated. Here is a request template for the IT team.

Request template for developers
Task: create a script/application for anonymizing 
procurement documents before sending to cloud LLMs.

Input: file (PDF, Word, Excel) with a commercial quotation.
Output: the same file with replaced data.

Replacement rules:
1. Company names → "Supplier A", "Supplier B", etc.
2. Amounts → relative ("base price", "+15%", "-20%")
3. Volumes → "small/medium/large batch"
4. Dates → relative ("in current quarter", "until end of year")
5. Contact details → remove completely
6. Contract numbers → "Contract N"

Additional: check — that the output file 
contains no original data (search by keywords).

Integration: drag-and-drop file → receive anonymized version 
in 5 seconds. Save anonymization history.

Priority: high (used daily by 5–10 procurement specialists).
💡 Tip: even if IT cannot build a full-fledged application, you can start with a simple Python script. It will replace 80% of manual work. Then refine it.

Knowledge Check

Question 1: Which of the following MUST NOT be sent to the cloud even after anonymization?

Supplier name → "Supplier A"
Specific penalty clauses from an active contract
"Price 10% above market"

Question 2: What is the first step of the anonymization checklist?

Remove all dates
Replace company names with "Supplier A", "Supplier B"
Remove all numbers from the document

Question 3: How do you check if a document is sufficiently anonymized?

Send to the cloud and see if the system blocks it
Show it to a colleague — they understand the gist, but don't know the supplier?
Delete all numbers and names — that's enough

Question 4: Which of the following is a hybrid task (anonymization + cloud)?

Checking a contract for deviations from a template
Role-play negotiation rehearsal with an anonymous scenario
Searching for new suppliers on the internet

Question 5: What should you do if procurement specialists constantly anonymize documents manually?

Prepare a request to developers for script automation
Continue manually — it's more reliable
Send originals to the cloud — it's faster
