AI for Non-Technical Specialists
Level 17 — HR

17.2 External Tasks: Cloud

⏱ 15 min · universal methodology without personal data
Main Lesson Principle
Only what is not personalized can be safely taken to external environment: methodology, templates, public labor market analytics.

Task 1: Job Postings and Position Descriptions

Why cloud: public content by definition. Job posting is published on job boards, social media, company website.

What AI Does

  • Formulates attractive description based on requirements
  • Adapts to company tone of voice
  • Adds ESG/DEI accents if needed
Prompt:
Create job description for position [title] in [industry]. Key requirements: [list]. Level: [junior/middle/senior]. Format: attractive, with accent on development and company culture. Add sections "What you'll do", "What we expect", "What we offer".
Result: saves 20–30 minutes per vacancy. HR can publish 2–3x more vacancies.

Task 2: Interview Questions Preparation (Methodology)

Why cloud: general methodology for position type — without reference to real candidate. Reusable content.

Prompt:
Develop behavioral interview structure for position [title] in [industry]. Level: [junior/middle/senior]. Include: opening questions, behavioral (STAR), case questions, culture fit questions, competency check questions [competency list].

Task 3: Standard Assessment Programs (Performance Review, 360)

Why cloud: assessment methodology — universal structure. Real assessments of specific employees — on-prem.

Prompt:
Develop 360 assessment program structure for [level: manager/specialist]. Include: competencies to assess, scale, respondent categories (manager, peers, subordinates), feedback format, timelines.

Task 4: Training Programs and Materials Development

Why cloud: general training materials — public part. Can be published on corporate portal.

Prompt:
Develop training program on topic [name] for employees level [junior/middle/senior]. Format: modular structure, 4–6 sessions of 2 hours each. For each module: objectives, content, methods, assessment. Include practical exercises.

Task 5: Tests and Assessment Materials Preparation

Why cloud: general-type tests — not tied to specific employee. Can be standard for all candidates for position.

Prompt:
Create test on knowledge of [subject/skill] for candidates for position [title]. 10 questions: 5 theoretical, 3 case-based, 2 logic. Specify correct answers and scoring criteria.

Task 6: General Onboarding Materials

Why cloud: materials about company, culture, processes — public part. Don't contain personal data.

Prompt:
Create onboarding guide for new employee. Sections: first day, first week, first month. Include: team introduction, key processes, tools, company culture, HR contact points. Format: friendly, structured.

Task 7: Salary Market Benchmarking

Why cloud: analysis of public salary surveys (job boards, Glassdoor, industry research) — open sources. Doesn't contain real employee salaries.

Prompt:
Based on open data (job boards, Glassdoor, industry surveys) for [period], analyze market salaries for position [title] in [region/industry]. Specify: median, P25, P75, P90. Compare with previous period. Highlight trends.

Task 8: Competency Matrices for Standard Positions

Why cloud: standard competencies — universal methodology. Tying to specific employee — on-prem.

Prompt:
Develop competency matrix for position [title]. Include: technical competencies, soft skills, leadership qualities (for managers). For each: description, development levels (1–5), behavior indicators. Format: table.

Task 9: Industry HR Practice Benchmarking

Why cloud: analysis of public reports, cases, research — open data.

Prompt:
Conduct HR practice benchmarking in [industry] for areas: recruitment, training, motivation, engagement. Use public reports from Deloitte, McKinsey, industry job boards. Highlight best practices and trends.

Task 10: General HR Strategy (Workforce Planning)

Why cloud: workforce planning methodology — universal. Real headcount figures — on-prem.

Prompt:
Develop HR strategy for company in [industry] for [period]. Include: current state analysis (typical problems), objectives, key initiatives (recruitment, development, retention), success metrics, risks. Format: structured plan.

Knowledge Check

Question 1: Why can onboarding materials be prepared in cloud?

They describe company and processes, not a specific employee
Because they are in PDF format
Because onboarding is a quick task

Question 2: Why can competency matrix be prepared in cloud?

It's universal methodology, not tied to a specific employee
Because matrix has levels 1–5
Because competencies are a public concept

Question 3: Which task from the list is NOT suitable for cloud?

360 assessment structure development
Analysis of real employees' 360 assessment results
Industry HR practice benchmarking
