0.1 · What are LLMs and how do they work?

🎯 No code · ✅ Quiz at the end

By 2026, companies that do not use AI will lose 20-40% productivity compared to competitors.

After this lesson you will be able to:
  • Explain to the director why AI is not “magic”, but a predictable tool
  • Understand which tasks are suitable for LLM and which are not
  • Ask the right questions to the IT team when choosing a solution

📚 What is LLM in simple words

LLM (Large Language Model) is a program that predicts the next word in a text.

💡 ANALOGY FOR NEGOTIATIONS

Think of the LLM as a trainee with a phenomenal memory. all books, articles and documents that humanity has created.

Like an experienced secretary who has seen thousands of letters and knows how they usually respond to a particular request.

How it works (without math)

  1. Education: The model “reads” billions of texts (books, articles, code, dialogues)
  2. Patterns: She remembers which words usually go together
  3. Prediction: When you ask a question, the model predicts the most likely answer
  4. Additional training (Fine-tuning): The creators "tweak" the model so that the answers are useful and safe
⚠️ Important limitation

LLM doesn't understand text just like a person. predicts.

🛠️ How to apply today

Checklist: is the task suitable for LLM?

  • Is the task related to the text (writing, analysis, summarization)?
  • Do you need a “creative” approach (options, ideas, formulations)?
  • Accuracy is not critical (can you check the result)?
  • No need for real, real-time data?
✅ SUITABLE FOR LLM
  • Summarizing a 50-page report into 1 page
  • Writing 10 versions of a letter to a supplier
  • Analysis of the contract for risky clauses
  • Translation of text into 5 languages
❌ NOT SUITABLE FOR LLM
  • Salary calculation accurate to the penny
  • Real price data for today (the model is “frozen” in time)
  • Legally binding opinions without verification
  • Diagnosis of diseases

👨‍💻 How to assign a task to a developer

Request template for IT: 
“We need to implement LLM for [OBJECTIVE].

✅ Checklist for accepting the result

  • Does the model cope with typical requests from your field?
  • Are the answers free of obvious errors (hallucinations)?
  • Is the response speed acceptable for the business process?
  • Is the cost of use within your budget?
  • Is there a process for human review of critical responses?

🧰 Tools for practice

Free neural networks (some):

  • ChatGPT (Free) — basic version of GPT-4o, message limit
  • Claude.ai — from Anthropic, works great with long documents
  • Kimi — kimi.moonshot.cn — from Moonshot AI, good for text processing, calculations, Chinese and English
  • Qwen — qwen.aliyun.com — from Alibaba, strong in multilingualism and versatility

Tools

  • GENAi (openwebui.com) — free interface, installs in 30 minutes
  • Qwen (Alibaba) — open-source model for commercial use

📝Checking understanding

Question 1: Does the LLM "understand" text the same way as a human?

A) No, LLM predicts probable words, is not conscious
B) Yes, the LLM understands context just like an expert
C) Only if it is GPT-4

Question 2: Can an LLM be trusted to calculate salaries?

A) Yes, LLM does great math.
B) No, accurate calculations are the weak point of LLM, you need a calculator
C) Only if you ask to show the steps

Question 3: Which is better for sensitive data?

A) ChatGPT - he is the smartest
B) On-premise (GENAi + local model) - data does not leave the company
C) Any paid version

🎯 The main thing from the lesson

LLM is a powerful tool for working with text, but it is not “magical intelligence.”
