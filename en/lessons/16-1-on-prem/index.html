Level 16 — Compliance

16.1 Internal Tasks: On-Prem

⏱ 25 min · for compliance officers, lawyers, CCO
Closed Loop (On-Prem)
All tasks in this lesson work with specific names, deals, transactions, personal data, whistleblower reports, investigations. On-prem is the default rule for compliance.

Why Compliance is One of the Most Closed Functions

Compliance combines sensitivity from several functions: like internal audit — vulnerability map and whistleblower risk, like legal — privilege and regulatory consequences, like security — investigations of specific people. Additional specificity: the very fact of "who exactly was checked and why" is sensitive information regardless of the check result.

When On-Prem is Mandatory:
  • Analysis of sanctions applicability to specific deal/counterparty
  • KYC/KYB — information collection on specific counterparties (beneficiaries, ownership structure)
  • Transaction analysis for suspicious activity signs (financial data)
  • SAR/STR preparation — reports on suspicious operations for regulator
  • Transaction monitoring alert investigation
  • Third-party due diligence for corruption risks (specific partners)
  • Gifts/entertainment expenses analysis (employee personal data)
  • Corruption signal investigation (jointly with internal audit)
  • Primary analysis of hotline reports (risk of whistleblower de-anonymization)
  • Investigation materials structuring, management reports
  • Compliance reporting for board of directors/audit committee
  • Responses to regulator inquiries
  • Preparation for external compliance audits (full picture of internal processes)
  • Compliance risk assessment applied to real business processes (vulnerability map)

1. Sanctions Applicability Analysis for Deal

Interpreting screening results for specific counterparty and deal — decision whether operation can proceed.

Prompt Example (On-Prem)
Analyze sanctions applicability to deal:

Deal data (confidential):
- Counterparty: [SPECIFY NAME]
- Counterparty country: [SPECIFY]
- Goods origin country: [SPECIFY]
- Goods/services type: [SPECIFY]
- Amount: [SPECIFY]
- Currency: [SPECIFY]
- Payment method: [SPECIFY]

Screening results (internal systems):
- OFAC: [SPECIFY]
- EU: [SPECIFY]
- UK: [SPECIFY]
- National lists: [SPECIFY]

Provide:
1. Deal risk assessment (high/medium/low/prohibited)
2. Applicable restrictive measures
3. Required additional checks
4. Conditions under which deal is possible (if possible)
5. Justification for compliance committee
6. Documentation trail (what to record)

Result: substantiated deal decision, saves 2–3 hours.

[SPECIFY ESSENCE (without source identification if whistleblower)]

6. Hotline Report Analysis

Primary categorization and prioritization of whistleblower reports.

Prompt Example
Analyze hotline report:

Report (anonymous):
[PASTE REPORT TEXT]

Provide:
1. Violation category (corruption/financial fraud/safety breach/discrimination/other)
2. Severity level (critical/high/medium/low)
3. Departments/persons mentioned in report
4. Required first investigation steps
5. Risks (retaliation, leak, reputation)
6. Investigation timeline recommendation
7. Priority relative to other cases

Important: preserve source anonymity.

Result: categorization and action plan, saves 1–2 hours.

Knowledge Check

Question 1: Why is primary whistleblower report analysis on-prem only?

Risk of whistleblower de-anonymization and compromising entire hotline system
Only if fewer than 10 reports per month
Can be in cloud if report text is anonymized

Question 2: SAR/STR — which environment?

On-prem — legally significant document with confidential data
External AI — regulators publish SAR requirements
Hybrid — structure in cloud, facts inside

Question 3: Why is KYC/KYB on-prem?

Beneficiaries and ownership structure — personal data
Public registries are available to everyone, so cloud is fine
Can be in cloud if names are anonymized
