10.3 Tasks in External AI: Cloud

20 min · for PR specialists · public content and open data

In this lesson — PR tasks that can be solved in external cloud AIs: Claude, ChatGPT, Kimi, Qwen, and others. We work with public information here: media, social networks, laws, ESG ratings, content for publication. No internal figures, strategies, or trade secrets.

Principle: if the data is already publicly available or intended for publication — feel free to use cloud AIs. They are better at understanding tone, style, languages, and trends.

What can be done in the cloud

PUBLIC Public content:
  • Media and social media publications (already published)
  • Press releases (being prepared for publication)
  • Texts of laws and regulations (open sources)
  • ESG ratings and competitor reports (publicly disclosed)
  • Social media posts (public content)
CLOUD Why cloud is suitable:
  • Better text generation and editing quality
  • Access to up-to-date knowledge about trends and events
  • Working with multilingual content
  • Fast processing of large volumes of public data

1. Monitoring company mentions in media and social networks

Collect links to publications, copy texts, or give the AI a list of URLs for analysis. The AI will extract key points, estimate reach, and compile a summary.

Prompt example
Here is a list of 15 publications about our company "AlphaStroy" for the past week (links and texts below).

Analyze each publication:
1. Media outlet / platform
2. Publication date
3. Key points (3–5 items)
4. Reach / audience (if specified)
5. Are there quotes from company management
6. Are there any negative claims

Create a summary table and a brief conclusion: what topics dominate the information field.
Input: links to articles, copied texts, screenshots with OCR.
Output: mention table + analytical summary.
Savings: 3–4 hours of manual review → 10 minutes.
Why outside: publications are already public, no confidential data.

2. Sentiment analysis of publications

Determine whether the media writes about your company positively, negatively, or neutrally. AI can distinguish hidden irony, veiled negativity, and objective facts.

Prompt example
Here are 8 publications about our company for the month (texts below).

Evaluate the sentiment of each publication on a scale:
- Positive (+1)
- Neutral (0)
- Negative (−1)
- Hidden negativity / irony (mark separately)

For each publication:
1. Specify the sentiment
2. Explain why you decided so (quote key phrases)
3. Assess the impact on reputation (high / medium / low)

Result: overall sentiment index for the month and trend (improvement / deterioration / stable).
Input: publication texts, links to articles.
Output: sentiment assessment for each publication + aggregate index.
Savings: 2–3 hours of analysis → 5 minutes.
Why outside: working with public texts, no internal data required.

3. Preparing press releases

Write facts, figures, quotes — AI will format a ready press release according to journalism standards. Then edit and publish.

Prompt example
Write a press release in Russian based on the following facts:

- Company: LLC "BetaLogistika"
- Event: opening of a new distribution center
- City: Kazan
- Area: 25,000 sq. m
- Investments: 1.2 billion rubles
- Jobs created: 350
- Launch: August 2026
- Quote from CEO Ivanov: "This is an important step in the Volga region coverage strategy"
- Additional: logistics complex certified to LEED Gold

Requirements:
- Headline (informative but catchy)
- Lead (key news in 1–2 sentences)
- Main text (facts, figures, context)
- Quote (in direct speech format)
- "About the company" block (2–3 sentences)
- Media contacts

Style: business, journalistic, no bureaucratic language.
Input: facts, figures, quotes, event context.
Output: ready press release according to standards.
Savings: 2–3 hours of writing → 10 minutes.
Why outside: press release is a public document intended for publication. Cloud AIs write better texts.

4. Social media management — post generation, content plan

Generate posts for different platforms, create content plans for a week/month. Consider the specifics of each platform's audience.

Prompt example
Our company is an eco-packaging manufacturer "GreenPack". Target audience: b2b buyers, retailers, ESG directors.

Create a weekly content plan for:
1. Company Telegram channel (business tone, long posts)
2. LinkedIn (international audience, in English)
3. VK (lighter tone, visual posts)

Weekly theme: launch of compostable packaging line.

For each post specify:
- Publication day
- Platform
- Topic
- Tone
- Post text (ready for publication)
- 3–5 hashtags

Additionally: write 2 headline variants for an email newsletter on the same topic.
Input: topic, company description, target audience, platforms.
Output: ready posts + content plan.
Savings: 1 day of copywriter work → 15 minutes.
Why outside: social media is a public platform, content is intended for a wide audience. Cloud AIs adapt style well to each platform.

5. Competitor monitoring in the information field

Track competitor publications: new products, M&A deals, management changes, crises. AI aggregates and structures information.

Prompt example
Here is a selection of publications about our 3 competitors for the last quarter (texts and links below):

Competitor 1: "StroyMontazh"
Competitor 2: "TeknoProm"
Competitor 3: "InzhStroy"

For each competitor analyze:
1. Number of media mentions
2. Main topics (new projects, scandals, awards)
3. Publication sentiment (positive / neutral / negative)
4. Key messages the company promotes
5. Changes in management or strategy (if any)

Create a comparison table and highlight:
- Which competitor is most active in media
- What topics they promote
- Where their weaknesses are (negativity, complaints)
Input: competitor publications, links, screenshots.
Output: comparison table + competitor activity analysis.
Savings: 4–5 hours of monitoring → 10 minutes.
Why outside: competitor information from open sources — public media, press releases.

6. Media digests for management

Daily or weekly prepare a selection of key industry news for top management. AI filters out noise and leaves only what matters.

Prompt example
Here are 25 news items from the construction materials industry for the week (texts below).

Compile a digest for company management (format: 1 A4 page):

1. Top-5 most important news (assess by market impact)
   - Headline
   - Essence in 2–3 sentences
   - Why this matters to us
2. Regulatory changes (laws, regulations)
3. Raw material price movements (if mentioned)
4. Competitor activity (if any)
5. ESG / sustainable development (news on ecology, certification)

Tone: business, no unnecessary details. Only what the CEO and CFO need to know.
Input: news selection, RSS feeds, copied texts.
Output: structured digest on 1 page.
Savings: 2–3 hours of reading and summarizing → 10 minutes.
Why outside: news from open sources, digest is an internal document but compiled from public data.

7. Analysis of legislative initiatives

Analyze draft laws, regulations, changes in regulation. AI will highlight key changes, deadlines, and business risks.

Prompt example
Here is the text of Federal Law Draft No. 12345 (on environmental regulation) and the explanatory note.

Analyze:
1. Brief essence of the bill (for a non-specialist)
2. Key changes compared to current legislation
3. Effective dates (if specified)
4. How this will affect our business (construction materials, manufacturing)
   - Obligations that will arise
   - Fine risks
   - Possible compliance costs
5. What needs to be done before the law takes effect

Format: 1-page business memo for management.
Input: bill text, explanatory note, links to regulation.gov.ru or State Duma.
Output: business memo with risks and recommendations.
Savings: 4–6 hours of legal analysis → 15 minutes.
Why outside: law texts are open data. Cloud AIs are good at legal language and can "translate" it into business language.

8. Monitoring ESG ratings and disclosure requirements

Track changes in ESG ratings, disclosure requirements, GRI, SASB, TCFD standards. AI aggregates changes from open sources.

Prompt example
Here is a selection of materials on changes in ESG regulation for the quarter:
- GRI Standard 2024 update
- New Central Bank of Russia requirements for ESG disclosure
- Changes in RAEX rating methodology
- EU Taxonomy update (green classification)

Analyze:
1. What exactly has changed (briefly, by item)
2. Effective dates
3. How this affects our disclosure obligations
4. What data we need to start collecting if not already
5. Recommendations: what to do in the next 3 months

Format: list with checklist for the ESG director.
Input: standard texts, regulator requirements, rating agency methodologies.
Output: change checklist + recommendations.
Savings: 1–2 days of studying standards → 15 minutes.
Why outside: ESG standards and methodologies are publicly available. Cloud AIs are well oriented in international standards.

9. Benchmarking competitor and industry PR activity

Compare your PR activity with competitors: publication frequency, topics, channels, sentiment. AI will process public data and build a comparison.

Prompt example
Here is data on PR activity of 5 industry companies for six months (collected from open sources):

Our company "AlphaStroy" + 4 competitors.

Data includes: number of publications, platforms, topics, sentiment.

Conduct benchmarking:
1. Publication frequency (per month) — comparison table
2. Top-3 topics for each company
3. Distribution channels (media, social networks, own platforms)
4. Average publication sentiment
5. Activity in ESG / sustainable development (number of mentions)
6. Where we lag behind leaders and where we lead

Conclusions: 3 recommendations for strengthening PR positions.
Input: competitor publication data (public sources).
Output: comparative PR activity analysis + recommendations.
Savings: 2–3 days of manual collection and analysis → 20 minutes.
Why outside: all data from open sources. Benchmarking is a comparison of public activity.

10. Draft ESG report sections (without internal operational figures)

Write the structure, introduction, approach descriptions, methodology. Internal figures (emissions, energy consumption) will be added later from internal systems.

Prompt example
Write draft sections of an ESG report for a manufacturing company "AlphaStroy" (construction materials).

Sections:
1. CEO introduction (2–3 paragraphs on sustainable development priority)
2. ESG approach description (what standards we use: GRI, SASB)
3. Environmental policy (general principles, no specific figures)
4. Social responsibility (approach to personnel, safety, training)
5. Corporate governance (structure, committees, ethics)
6. Data collection methodology (how we count emissions, energy, incidents)
7. Plans for next year (improvement directions)

Rules:
- Do not use specific operational figures (leave placeholders like [INSERT: CO2 emissions])
- Style: official but understandable
- Compliance with GRI Universal Standards 2021
- Length: 5–7 pages of finished text
Input: company description, applied standards, general principles.
Output: ESG report draft with placeholders for figures.
Savings: 3–5 days of copywriter work → 20 minutes.
Why outside: structure, introduction, and methodology do not contain confidential data. Internal figures are added later in a closed circuit.

Separation rule: what goes in cloud, what stays inside

CLOUD Do in cloud AIs:
  • Everything already published or being prepared for publication
  • Analysis of public data (media, laws, ratings)
  • Text generation for a wide audience
  • Translations and content adaptation for different markets
ON-PREM Keep inside:
  • Internal communications and official correspondence
  • Strategic plans and crisis scenarios
  • Specific operational data for reports (emissions, incidents)
  • Negotiating position on disputed issues

Knowledge Check

Question 1: Why can press release preparation be done in cloud AI?

Because press releases contain trade secrets
Because a press release is a public document intended for publication
Because AI cannot work with internal data

Question 2: Which task from the list is NOT suitable for cloud AI?

Sentiment analysis of media publications
Developing crisis communication strategy with specific scenarios
Monitoring competitor ESG ratings

Question 3: Why can an ESG report draft be started in the cloud?

Because all ESG report data is public by definition
Because structure, introduction, and methodology do not contain confidential operational figures
Because ESG standards prohibit the use of internal systems

Question 4: Which of the following CAN be sent to cloud ChatGPT?

Internal memo about a reputational crisis with an action plan
Correspondence with a lawyer about a lawsuit against the company
Text of a published media article for sentiment analysis
