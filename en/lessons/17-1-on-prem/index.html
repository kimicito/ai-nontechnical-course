AI for Non-Technical Specialists
Level 17 — HR

17.1 Internal Tasks: On-Prem

⏱ 20 min · tasks involving a specific person
Main Lesson Principle
HR works with personal data in almost every task. Rule: if the text contains a name, salary, evaluation, or personal situation of a specific person — on-prem only.

Task 1: Resume Screening and Candidate Assessment

Why on-prem: resumes contain personal data (name, contacts, previous employers, salary). Even matching analysis is personal data processing.

What AI Does

  • Compares resume with vacancy requirements
  • Highlights candidate strengths and weaknesses
  • Creates brief annotation for HR
Prompt:
Vacancy requirements: [requirements text].
Candidate resume: [resume text].

Analyze match by each requirement. Highlight strengths, risks, interview questions. Create brief annotation (max 150 words).
Result: saves 10–15 minutes per resume. A team of 3 HR can process 2x more candidates.

Task 2: HR Orders Preparation

Why on-prem: orders contain names, salaries, positions, dates — full set of personal data. Legally significant documents.

What AI Does

  • Generates order text by template
  • Checks compliance with labor code
  • Forms attachments list
Prompt:
Create employment order. Data: name [name], position [title], department [name], salary [amount], start date [date], probation period [period]. Check compliance with labor code. Specify required attachments.

Task 3: Personnel Files Management and Structuring

Why on-prem: personnel files are personal data in the full sense. Even text part contains biographical information.

Prompt:
Structure the following documents in personnel file [name] into standardized format: [document list]. For each document specify date, type, compliance with HR record-keeping requirements.

Task 4: HR Reporting (Turnover, Staffing)

Why on-prem: analytics by department, names of departed employees, reasons for leaving — management-sensitive information.

Prompt:
Turnover data for [period]: [data table]. Analyze: trends by department, average tenure, main reasons for leaving (by categories). Create summary for management with recommendations.

Task 5: Personnel Assessment (Performance Review)

Why on-prem: assessment of real employees — extremely sensitive information. Affects career, compensation, motivation of specific people.

What AI Does

  • Structures feedback from 360 reviews
  • Creates competency summary
  • Prepares phrasing for review meeting
Prompt:
360 review results for employee [name] for [period]: [evaluation data]. Structure feedback by competencies. Highlight strengths, growth areas, recommendations. Prepare phrasing for review meeting.

Task 6: Individual Development Plans (IDP)

Why on-prem: IDP is tied to specific employee, their assessment, career goals.

Prompt:
Create IDP for employee [name] for [period]. Data: current position [title], assessment results [data], employee goals [goals]. Include: competencies to develop, activities, timelines, success criteria, manager support.

Task 7: Talent Pool and Succession Programs

Why on-prem: "who is targeting which position" is politically sensitive information inside the company. Leakage can cause conflicts and key employee turnover.

Prompt:
Based on potential matrix and key employee assessment: [anonymized data — no names, only IDs], form talent pool proposals for critical positions. Specify: positions, required competencies, preparation timelines, succession absence risks.

Task 8: Payroll Justification and Grading

Why on-prem: real salaries, grades of specific positions — confidential information. AI processes text justification part, not mathematical calculation.

Prompt:
Justify grading proposal for position [title]. Data: current grade [number], market median [range], internal fairness (ratio with similar roles). Formulate arguments for management.

Task 9: Exit Interview Analysis

Why on-prem: exit interviews may contain criticism of management, personal reasons, complaints — all personal data + de-anonymization risk.

Prompt:
Analyze reasons for leaving based on exit interviews for [period]: [interview texts, anonymized]. Group reasons by categories. Highlight systemic problems and recommendations for resolution.

Task 10: Disciplinary Actions

Why on-prem: legally significant documents with personal data. Require precise wording.

Prompt:
Draft disciplinary action order. Data: name [name], position [title], violation [description], date [date], witnesses [list]. Check compliance with labor code articles. Specify legal grounds.

Task 11: Labor Disputes, Employer Position

Why on-prem: specific employee, their labor dispute, company defense strategy — attorney-client privilege + trade secret.

Prompt (jointly with lawyer):
Situation: employee [name] filed claim [claim essence]. Facts: [context]. Prepare employer position: legal justification, risks, possible resolution scenarios. Identify weak points in position.

Task 12: Harassment/Discrimination Complaints

Why on-prem: maximum sensitivity. Risk of complainant de-anonymization, secondary trauma, reputational risks for company.

Prompt:
Create plan for initial processing of [type: harassment/discrimination] complaint. Include: investigation stages, required documents, confidentiality requirements, timelines, final document format. Specify what requires external lawyer involvement.

Task 13: HR Metrics Reports for Management

Why on-prem: management information: payroll, turnover by department, labor productivity — strategic data.

Prompt:
Based on HR metrics for [period]: [data], create analytical summary for management. Include: key trends, deviations from plan, risks, recommendations. Format: 1 page, structured, with conclusions.

Task 14: Reference Check of Specific Candidates

Why on-prem: reference check includes personal data of not only candidate but third parties (recommenders, former managers).

Prompt:
Reference check results for candidate [name]: [conversation notes/recommendation letters]. Structure information by categories: professional qualities, communication, reliability, growth areas. Create summary for decision-making.

Knowledge Check

Question 1: Why is even a simple employee reference letter on-prem?

Contains personal data (name, position, salary)
Because it's an internal document
Because references are rarely requested

Question 2: Which HR task is most sensitive among listed?

Performance review
Harassment/discrimination complaints
Talent pool and succession

Question 3: Why is salary benchmarking an external AI task, not on-prem?

Analyzes public salary surveys, not specific employee salaries
Because methodology is universal
Because external AI is more accurate in calculations
