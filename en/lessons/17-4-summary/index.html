Level 17 — HR

17.4 Practical Summary

⏱ 15 min · level summary
Main Principle of Level 17
HR — function with the widest personal data surface of all covered. Almost any task touches a specific person. Rule: if the text contains a name, salary, evaluation, or personal situation — on-prem only.

Key Takeaways

1. On-Prem — Default Rule

For HR, on-prem is not an exception but the norm:

  • Resume screening — candidates' personal data
  • HR orders — names, salaries, legally significant documents
  • Personnel files, references — personal data in full sense
  • HR reporting — turnover by department, management sensitivity
  • Personnel assessment, IDP — affects careers of specific people
  • Talent pool — politically sensitive information
  • Exit interviews — may contain criticism of management
  • Disciplinary actions, labor disputes — legally significant documents
  • Harassment complaints — maximum sensitivity
  • Reference check — third parties' personal data

2. External AI — Non-Personalized Only

Safe tasks for cloud:

  • Job postings (public content)
  • Interview methodology (without candidate reference)
  • Standard assessment programs (structure)
  • General training programs
  • General-type tests
  • Onboarding materials (about company, culture)
  • Salary market benchmarking (public surveys)
  • Competency matrices (standard)
  • HR practice benchmarking (public reports)
  • General HR strategy (methodology)

3. Hybrid — for Templates and Methodology

Five tasks work on hybrid scheme:

  • Reference check — structure outside, candidate assessment inside
  • IDP — template outside, employee tie inside
  • Engagement surveys — questions outside, answer analysis inside
  • Feedback — principles outside, employee facts inside
  • Offers/contracts — template outside, real conditions inside

4. Unique Factor — Data Protection Legislation

HR differs from other functions in being regulated not only by trade secrets but also by personal data protection legislation (GDPR, local equivalents). This creates additional legal risk when using external AI.

What's Excluded from Level (and Why)

Automated resume scoring by ML model — ATS systems (Applicant Tracking Systems) use ranking algorithms for preliminary resume sorting. This is not generative AI but supervised ML on HR data. GenAI is connected only to interpretation and text formatting of such systems' results.

Predictive turnover analytics — supervised ML models on historical HR data. GenAI can help in formatting conclusions but not in building the model itself.

Checklist for HR Before External AI

Before sending request to external AI, check:

  • ❌ No employee/candidate names?
  • ❌ No salaries, grades?
  • ❌ No specific people's evaluations?
  • ❌ No department names (if could de-anonymize)?
  • ❌ No complaints/appeals with details?
  • ❌ No exit interview results with quotes?
  • ❌ No strategic HR decisions (layoffs, reorganizations)?

If any item fails — on-prem only.

Knowledge Check — Final Quiz

Question 1: Why is HR the function with the widest personal data surface?

Almost any task touches a specific employee or candidate
Because HR works with paperwork
Because company has many employees

Question 2: Can performance review be done in external AI?

Yes, if using general assessment scale
No, it's assessment of a specific person with personal data
Yes, if assessment is anonymous (360)

Question 3: Why are harassment complaints on-prem only?

Because they are legally significant documents
Risk of complainant de-anonymization and secondary trauma
Because external lawyer involvement is required
