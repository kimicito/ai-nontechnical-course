7.1 Internal Tasks: On-Prem

15 min · for procurement specialists · closed loop

This lesson covers tasks that a procurement specialist performs inside the company, with data that cannot be sent to the internet. Prices, terms, negotiation position — all of this is a trade secret.

Solution: on-prem LLM (e.g., Qwen) within the company's closed perimeter. Data never leaves the perimeter.

Why On-Prem for Procurement

What cannot be sent to the cloud:
  • Specific supplier prices from quotations
  • Contract terms and penalties
  • Negotiation position (BATNA, leverage)
  • Purchase volumes by item
  • Historical supplier data
What can be done on-prem:
  • Compare 5 quotations by price, lead time, and terms — in 2 minutes
  • Check whether a proposal matches the technical specification
  • Prepare arguments for negotiations
  • Find deviations in a contract from the template
  • Analyze procurement patterns over 2 years

1. Comparative Quotation Analysis

A procurement specialist receives 3–5 commercial quotations. Before: manually compile in Excel, compare price, delivery time, payment terms, warranties. Now: upload to on-prem LLM and ask a question.

Prompt example
I have 3 commercial quotations for pump supply.
Files Q1, Q2, Q3 are attached.

Create a comparison table:
- Price per unit
- Delivery time
- Payment terms
- Warranty period
- Shipping cost

Highlight the best offer for each criterion and the overall winner.
Result: comparison table + visual highlighting of the best options. The procurement specialist saves 20–30 minutes on routine work.

2. Proposal vs. Technical Specification Check

Often a supplier responds to a technical specification, but with caveats or deviations. An LLM can find these deviations faster than a human.

Prompt example
Compare the technical specification (file TS.docx) and the supplier's response (file Response.docx).

Find:
1. Full deviations from the TS (what does not match)
2. Partial deviations (what matches with caveats)
3. Missing items (what the supplier did not address)

Make a list with reference to specific TS clauses.

3. Negotiation Preparation

This is the most sensitive task. BATNA (best alternative to a negotiated agreement), leverage, supplier weaknesses — none of this can be exposed in the cloud. On-prem solves the problem.

Prompt example
I am preparing for negotiations with supplier X for equipment procurement.

Here is my data:
- There are 2 alternative suppliers with prices 15% lower
- The current supplier delayed delivery by 2 weeks last quarter
- I need delivery in 3 weeks, but their standard is 6 weeks
- Order volume is 50 units, which is a large order for their category

Prepare:
1. My arguments for price reduction
2. Leverage points (polite but convincing)
3. BATNA — what to do if we don't agree
4. Negotiation scenario (step by step)

4. Contract Review

A lawyer reviews a contract for a week. An LLM finds deviations from the template in minutes — the lawyer then validates the findings.

Prompt example
Compare the supplier's contract (file Contract.docx) and our standard template (file Template.docx).

Find deviations in:
- Liability of the parties
- Penalties
- Delivery terms
- Payment terms
- Dispute resolution procedure

For each deviation indicate: where in the template, where in the contract, risk for us.

5. SKU / Nomenclature Normalization

In a company, 5 departments call the same product differently: "pump Grundfos 32-160", "pump Gründfos 32-160", "GRUNDFOS 32-160". An LLM brings them to a unified form.

Prompt example
Here is a nomenclature list from 3 departments (file Nomenclature.xlsx).

Bring to a unified form:
- Standardize brand names (Latin)
- Standardize units of measure
- Remove duplicates
- Create a mapping table: old name → new name

Save the result in Excel.

6. Procurement History Analysis

A procurement specialist wants to understand: are prices rising with a regular supplier? Is there seasonality? Which items are getting more expensive the fastest?

Prompt example
I have 2 years of procurement history (file History.xlsx).

Analyze:
1. Price dynamics for the top 10 suppliers (trend up/down)
2. Seasonality (which months are more expensive)
3. Top 5 items with the highest price increase
4. Average time between order and delivery by supplier
5. Recommendations: where there is a risk of price increases, what to watch

7. Supplier Risk Scoring

A combination of internal supplier data + risk assessment template. The LLM calculates a score and explains why.

Prompt example
Assess the risk of supplier "PromTekhSnab" based on the data:

- Working together for 3 years
- 2 delivery delays in the last year (5 and 12 days)
- 1 quality claim (defect 3%, fixed at their expense)
- Price is 8% below market
- Financials: revenue growing, no litigation
- Contract until year-end, then retender

On a scale of 1 to 10:
1. Overall risk profile
2. Operational risk (deadline failure)
3. Financial risk
4. Quality risk

Give a recommendation: extend, retender, or find a backup.

8. Transport Document Processing

Waybills, invoices, CMR — an LLM extracts data and cross-checks against the order. All inside the perimeter.

Prompt example
File Waybill.pdf — parse the contents:

- Waybill number, date
- Shipper and consignee
- List of goods with quantity and weight
- Value of goods
- Shipment date and delivery date (actual vs. planned)

Cross-check against the order (file Order.xlsx) and identify:
- Shortages
- Wrong items
- Deviations in delivery time

9. Management Reports

A procurement specialist spends the evening on PowerPoint. An LLM drafts a report in 10 minutes — only the numbers need to be verified.

Prompt example
Based on data from file Purchases_Q3.xlsx, prepare a draft report for the board of directors:

1. Total procurement volume (amount, growth vs. Q2)
2. Top 5 categories by amount
3. Status of key projects (on time / at risk / overdue)
4. Recommendations for 2–3 problem areas
5. Q4 plan (brief)

Format: structured text, ready to paste into a presentation.

Question 1: Why can't quotation comparison be sent to cloud ChatGPT?

Because supplier prices are a trade secret
Because ChatGPT can't read Excel

Question 2: What tool should be used to prepare for negotiations with BATNA?

Google Docs
On-prem LLM in a closed loop
ChatGPT with data deletion after

Question 3: How much time does an LLM save on comparing 5 quotations?

It doesn't save, manual is faster
30 minutes → 2 minutes
5 minutes → 4 minutes

Question 4: What is supplier risk scoring?

Risk assessment on a 1–10 scale based on internal data
Credit history check at a bank
Online search for supplier reviews

Question 5: Which of these documents definitely belongs in on-prem?

Draft TS without prices
Contract with specific penalties and terms
Translation of an instruction from English
