Level 12 — MRO (Maintenance, Repair, and Operations)

12.1 Internal MRO Tasks

⏱ 20 min · for reliability engineers and mechanics
Closed Loop (on-prem)
All tasks in this lesson work with specific equipment, real failures, financial data. Use an internal model or local LLM.

Why On-Prem is Critical for MRO

MRO data is not just spare parts lists. It is:

  • Condition of real assets — defect reports describe wear of specific machines
  • Production vulnerability — failure history reveals weak points in production lines
  • Financial obligations — work completion certificates, basis for contractor payments
  • Competitive information — downtime, repair schedules, budgets
When On-Prem is Mandatory:
  • Defect reports for specific equipment
  • Root-cause analysis with real failure data
  • Spare parts specifications with plant nomenclature
  • Failure history and MTBF (internal reliability)
  • Repair documentation (certificates, reports) — financially significant
  • Correspondence with suppliers (commercial terms)
  • Claims work (legally significant)
  • Interpretation of vibration diagnostics/NDT for specific units
  • Reliability reports for management
  • Downtime and cause analysis (may involve personnel)
  • Repair budget justification
  • "Repair vs Replace" justification — investment decision
  • Analysis of contractor proposals for overhaul

1. Development and Updating of Maintenance Schedules

Planned preventive maintenance (PPM) is the foundation of planning. LLM helps update schedules based on actual data.

Prompt Example (on-prem)
Update the PPM schedule for Workshop #3 for Q3 2026:

Source data:
- Current schedule: Major repair of Mill M-3 (August), Medium repair of compressors (July, September)
- Actual: Compressor K-05 failed on 2026-07-10, unplanned repair required
- Resource: mechanic team — 6 people, availability — 80%
- Constraints: August — vacation period, 2 people on leave

Do:
1. Adjust the schedule considering unplanned repair of K-05
2. Redistribute work between July and September
3. Assess team workload (person/hours) by month
4. Risks: what will shift if more unplanned work appears
5. Recommendations for capacity reserve

Result: adjusted PPM schedule, saves 2–3 hours.

2. Creating Defect Reports

After equipment inspection — systematize identified defects into a standard document.

Prompt Example (on-prem LLM)
Based on inspection data of electric motor EM-37 (Workshop #3):
- Bearing NDE vibration: 8.2 mm/s (norm ≤4.5)
- Winding temperature: 127°C (norm ≤105)
- Bearing oil: darkened, contaminants
- Shaft play: 0.35 mm (norm ≤0.1)

Create a defect report in the standard form:
1. Equipment name, inventory number
2. Identified defects (list)
3. Criticality assessment (critical / significant / minor)
4. Recommended actions
5. Resolution deadline
6. Responsible department

Result: ready defect report, saves 30 min on documentation.

3. Root-Cause Analysis of Failures (RCA, 5 Why)

Investigation of failure causes tied to a specific equipment unit.

Prompt Example
Failure: Pump P-12 (slurry transfer, calcination workshop) stopped on 2026-07-15 03:20.

Data:
- Operating hours since last repair: 4,847 h
- Previous failure: 2025-11-03, bearing replacement
- Current parameters before stop: vibration 12.4 mm/s, temperature 89°C
- Visual inspection: impeller damage, scoring on bushing

Conduct root-cause analysis:
1. 5 Why (5 levels of "why")
2. Ishikawa diagram (6M: Man, Machine, Material, Method, Milieu, Measurement)
3. Root cause of failure
4. Corrective actions (short-term)
5. Preventive actions (long-term)
6. Recommendations for maintenance schedule changes

Result: structured investigation, saves 2–3 hours.

4. Preparation of Spare Parts Specifications

For a specific repair — list of required materials and spare parts tied to nomenclature.

Prompt Example
Major repair of gearbox G-08 (Mill M-3 drive) is required.

Based on defect inspection:
- Bearings 22320 (2 pcs.) — worn, replacement
- Intermediate shaft — journal wear (requires build-up + grinding)
- Cylindrical gears — scoring on teeth (replacement)
- Seals 80×100×10 (4 pcs.)
- Oil I-40A — 45 L (flush + fill)

Prepare spare parts and materials specification:
1. Item, name, drawing/GOST
2. Quantity, unit
3. Enterprise nomenclature code (if available)
4. Recommended supplier (from approved list)
5. Estimated price per unit
6. Delivery time
7. Flag: stock / to order

Add notes on replacement (e.g., analogs if original unavailable).

Result: ready specification for procurement, saves 1 hour.

5. Analysis of Failure History and MTBF

Calculation of mean time between failures and identification of patterns.

Prompt Example
Failure history of Compressor K-05 (preparation workshop):
- 2024-03-12: suction valve failure, operating time 3,200 h
- 2024-09-08: bearing overheating, operating time 2,850 h
- 2025-02-20: seal damage, operating time 2,100 h
- 2025-08-15: connecting rod breakage, operating time 2,400 h
- 2026-01-30: discharge valve wear, operating time 2,650 h
- 2026-07-10: bearing overheating (repeat), operating time 2,200 h

Analyze:
1. MTBF for the period (average operating time between failures)
2. MTBF trend (improving / deteriorating / stable)
3. Frequent failures (which components fail most often)
4. Recommendations for maintenance schedule changes
5. Forecast: when to expect next failure

Result: statistical analysis for planning, saves 1.5 hours.

6. Preparation of Terms of Reference for Overhaul

Terms of Reference — main document for contractor. Contains scope of work, technical requirements, deadlines.

Prompt Example (on-prem)
Prepare ToR for major repair of Mill M-3 (inv. # M-03-2012):

Technical data:
- Commissioning year: 2012, operating time: 87,400 h
- Last major repair: 2021
- Type: ball mill 3.2×15 m, capacity 120 t/h
- Drive: gearbox G-08, motor 800 kW

Scope of work (based on defect inspection):
1. Replacement of lining (set, ~45 t)
2. Repair/replacement of gearbox G-08
3. Replacement of lubrication system (pumps, pipelines)
4. Repair of end seals
5. Testing (idle run, under load)
6. Acceptance criteria (vibration ≤4.5 mm/s, temperature ≤65°C)

Requirements:
- Execution time: 21 calendar days
- Warranty: 18 months
- Documentation: concealed works certificates, material certificates, photo report

Result: ready ToR for tender, saves 3–4 hours.

8. Correspondence with Suppliers and Service Companies

Negotiation of spare parts delivery terms, service maintenance deadlines.

Prompt Example
Prepare a letter to supplier LLC "TekhnoSnab":

Subject: Clarification of delivery time for bearings 22320 for gearbox G-08

Context:
- Order placed on 2026-06-20 (request # Z-1847)
- Promised delivery: 4 weeks
- Current status: not received, deadline approaching (repair scheduled for 2026-08-05)
- Alternative: SKF bearings of similar type (clarify compatibility)

Tone: business-like, urgent but non-confrontational. Mention possibility of contract penalties.

Result: ready letter, saves 20 min.

9. Claims Work for Warranty Cases

Justification of claims against supplier or contractor for warranty failure.

Prompt Example
Prepare a claim for a warranty case:

Equipment: Fan VO-16, supplier LLC "VentProm"
Contract: # 78/2025 dated 10.03.2025
Warranty period: 18 months
Commissioning date: 2025-06-15

Defect: impeller blade breakage (2026-07-08)
Cause (preliminary assessment): casting defect (porosity in mounting zone)
Operating time at failure: 8,420 h (within warranty)

Requirements:
1. Free replacement of impeller
2. Compensation for dismantling/installation costs
3. Compensation for downtime losses (2 shifts)

Attachments: inspection report, defect photos, laboratory conclusion.

Result: legally competent claim, saves 1–1.5 hours.

10. Interpretation of Vibration Diagnostics and NDT Reports

Textual analysis of ready reports — not a replacement for DSP/ML, but help in formulating conclusions.

Prompt Example
Interpret the vibration diagnostics report for Pump P-12:

Data:
- Measurement point: NDE bearing, radial vibration
- Values (mm/s): overall — 8.2, 1× — 5.1, 2× — 1.8, 3× — 0.4, BPF — 0.3
- 3-month trend: growth from 4.8 to 8.2 mm/s
- Bearing temperature: 67°C (norm ≤65)

Draw conclusions:
1. What the spectrum shows (dominant frequency)
2. Probable cause (unbalance / bearing defect / cavitation / etc.)
3. Recommendations (urgency, actions)
4. Forecast: how many hours until critical level

Result: structured interpretation, saves 30 min.

11. Reliability Reports for Management

Management summary on equipment condition and trends.

Prompt Example
Prepare a monthly reliability report for Workshop #3 equipment for June 2026:

Data:
- Total equipment units: 147
- Failures: 12 (8 in May)
- Planned repairs: 5
- Unplanned stops: 7
- Total downtime: 34 hours
- Average MTBF: 2,340 h (May: 2,680 h)
- Critical failures: 2 (compressor K-05, pump P-12)

Format: 1 A4 page for production director.
Structure: key metrics, trends, problem areas, recommendations.

Result: ready report, saves 1 hour.

12. Downtime and Cause Analysis

Distribution of downtime by types, equipment, causes — for decision making.

Prompt Example
Downtime analysis for Workshop #3 for Q2 2026:

Data (in hours):
- Equipment: pumps — 28, compressors — 45, conveyors — 12, mills — 38
- Causes: wear — 67, operator error — 18, external factors — 14, other — 24
- Shifts: day — 61, night — 62

Conduct analysis:
1. Pareto distribution (80/20) — what causes main downtime
2. Trend vs Q1
3. Risk zones (equipment with growing downtime)
4. Recommendations (maintenance, replacement, training)

Result: analytics for planning, saves 1.5 hours.

13. Repair vs Replacement Justification

Investment decision — repair old or buy new.

Prompt Example
Justify the decision: repair or replace Compressor K-05?

Data:
- Production year: 2012 (14 years of operation)
- Major repair cost: 8.5 mln ₽ (forecast)
- New analog cost: 32 mln ₽
- MTBF after major repair (history): ~2,800 h
- MTBF of new: ~8,000 h
- Energy consumption of old: 18% higher than new
- Annual spare parts cost: 2.1 mln ₽
- Residual value of old: 0

Prepare comparative analysis (3 years, 5 years, 10 years), considering:
- CAPEX (capital expenditures)
- OPEX (operation, spare parts, energy)
- Downtime (production loss)
- Risks (uncertainty of MTBF after major repair)

Result: justified decision with numbers, saves 3–4 hours.

14. Budget Justification for Repairs

Financial justification of necessary repair expenses.

Prompt Example
Prepare budget justification for Workshop #3 repairs for 2027:

Source data:
- Actual 2026: 47.3 mln ₽ (plan: 42 mln, overspend 12.6%)
- Main overspend items: unplanned compressor failures (+8.2 mln), conveyor C-04 replacement (+4.1 mln)
- Planned repairs 2027: Major repair of Mill M-3 (15 mln), Medium repair of compressors (12 mln), current repair (18 mln)
- Reserve for unplanned: 8 mln
- Inflation/parts price increase: +8%

Total: 57.8 mln ₽ (+22% vs 2026)

Prepare justification for CFO: why +22%, what happens if cut, what risks.

Result: justification for board of directors, saves 2–3 hours.

15. Analysis of Contractor Proposals for Overhaul

Comparison of commercial proposals by price/deadlines/experience criteria.

Prompt Example
ToR: major repair of Mill M-3 (lining replacement, gearbox, lubrication system).

Contractor proposals:
1. LLC "MetallRemont" — 15.2 mln, 21 days, 5 similar objects, warranty 12 mos.
2. LLC "GorTekhServis" — 13.8 mln, 28 days, 12 objects, warranty 18 mos.
3. LLC "SibRemStroy" — 14.5 mln, 18 days, 3 objects, warranty 6 mos.

Criteria (weight):
- Price (30%), deadline (25%), experience (20%), warranty (15%), reviews (10%)

Prepare comparison table and recommendation.

Result: justified contractor choice, saves 2 hours.

Knowledge Check

Question 1: Why is analysis of contractor proposals for overhaul on-prem?

Commercial terms of competitors, prices, deadlines — confidential
Because ToR contains secret blueprints of equipment
Because spare parts need to be found on the open market

Question 2: Can you prepare an MOP maintenance procedure for a pump in external AI?

Yes, if it is a general structure for a typical pump without plant-specific data
No, any MRO document contains production secrets
Only if you anonymize the plant name

Question 3: "Repair vs Replace" justification — which loop?

On-prem — contains financial data, production parameters, risks
Cloud — TCO calculation methodology is publicly available
Hybrid — methodology outside, numbers inside
