1.1 · Prompt engineering for a manager

🎯 No code · ✅ Interactive quiz

🎯 Why does business need this?

80% of the result from AI depends not on the model, but on how you asked it.

The manager spends on average 2-3 hours a day for: writing letters, analyzing documents, comparing proposals, preparing reports.

Saving example:

Comparing 3 vendor quotes takes 2 hours manually. 1 hour 45 minutes × 10 times a month = 17.5 hours.

📚 What we are studying

Formula for a good prompt: RCOFE

LetterMeaningQuestion
RRoleWho is responsible?
CContextWhat's happening?
OObjectiveWhat to get?
FFormatIn what form?
EEval (Self-Check)Verify the result: "Check that all columns are filled, no illogical cost differences"

Comparison: bad vs good prompt

❌ Bad prompt
"Analyze the proposals and tell me which is better"

Result: a general answer without specifics, you will have to ask again 3 times

✅ Good prompt (RCOFE)
You are an experienced FMCG buyer with 10 years of experience.

🛠️ How to apply

Step 1: Define the role

AI responds better when it knows who it is “pretending to be.”

  • "You're a lawyer"— legal analysis, careful wording
  • "You're a marketer"— creative, metrics, CTA
  • "You're a financial analyst"— numbers, ROI, risks

Step 2: Give Context

Without context, AI gives a general answer.

💡 Example

Without context:"How to reduce costs?"

With context:“We are a cookie manufacturer, 50 employees, logistics takes up 30% of the budget. How to reduce costs?”

Step 3: Specify the format

The format saves time on reformatting.

FormatWhen to useExample request
TableComparison, analysis"Output in the table: Criterion | Option 1 | Option 2"
ListStep by step plan"Give 5 steps as a numbered list"
JSONFor programmers/automation"Return the result in JSON with fields..."
EmailReady letter"Write a letter to the supplier in a business style"

Step 4: Add self-check (Eval)

Eval is a request for AI to check its answer before outputting. This reduces errors and hallucinations by 30-50%.

💡 Examples of Eval
Check that all columns in the table are filled.
Check that there are no illogical cost differences.
Check that all numbers add up correctly.
If any item is not specified in the document — write "Not specified".

Why do it: AI sometimes "hallucinates" missing data or skips a row in a table. Eval forces it to double-check — like asking a colleague to "check again".

👨‍💻 How to delegate to a developer

Developer task template

"We need to create a library of prompts for the [NAME] department.

✅ Checklist for accepting the result

  • Does the prompt contain all 5 RCOFE elements?
  • Is the role appropriate for the task?
  • Is the context specific enough?
  • Is the goal clear?
  • Is the format convenient for copying to Excel/Word/CRM?
  • Are there any restrictions?
  • Have you checked it with 3 real examples?

🎯 The main thing from the lesson

Prompt is TK for AI. Use the RCOFE formula: Role, Context, Purpose, Format, Eval. The more specific the prompt — the better the result. A bad prompt wastes your time on clarification. A good prompt saves 2-3 hours a day.
