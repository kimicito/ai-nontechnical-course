🎯 Prompt Examples for Different Professions

Ready-to-use templates for real tasks. Copy, paste into GENAi (or any AI chat), replace [text in brackets] with your data.

📋 How to Use These Examples

Each prompt is built using the RCOFE formula (Role-Context-Objective-Format-Evaluation) and adapted for on-premise solutions like GENAi:

  • No API, programming, or JSON required
  • Works in any chat interface (OpenWebUI, ChatGPT, Claude)
  • For confidential documents, use GENAi inside your company
  • Always verify the output before sending to a client / to court / to reporting

HR Manager
Recruitment, onboarding, HR brand
Create a new employee onboarding plan
Role: You are an HR manager with experience in employee onboarding at an IT company. Task: Create a 5-day onboarding plan for [position, e.g., "sales manager"]. Context: - Company: [name/description] - Work format: [office/hybrid/remote] - Key tools: [list: CRM, corporate chat, etc.] - Mentor: [name/position] Format: Table with columns: Day | Time | Activity | Responsible | Goal | Result Evaluation: Check that all key processes (access, team introduction, first tasks) are distributed logically across days.
Prepare an FAQ on a new policy for employees
Role: You are an HR communicator. You can explain complex policies in simple language. Task: Write an FAQ for employees on the topic: [topic, e.g., "new remote work policy"]. Context: - What changed: [brief description of changes] - Top 5 questions from employees: [list] - Tone: warm, supportive, without bureaucratic jargon Format: 7-10 questions with answers. Each answer — no more than 3 sentences. At the end — where to go with additional questions. Evaluation: Check that answers do not contradict each other and cover the main scenarios.
Write an internal job posting announcement
Role: You are an HR brand manager. You write job postings that people want to apply for. Task: Create a description for an internal vacancy for the position [position]. Context: - Department: [name] - Key tasks: [3-5 items] - Requirements: [mandatory and preferred] - Why it's great: [2-3 unique features of the role/project] Format: Brief introduction (1 paragraph) → Responsibilities (list) → Requirements (list) → What we offer (list) → How to apply. Evaluation: Check that the text sounds lively, without clichés like "dynamic, fast-growing company."

Lawyer
Contracts, risks, compliance
Perform a preliminary contract risk review
Role: You are a corporate lawyer with experience in [industry, e.g., "IT/telecom"]. Task: Perform a preliminary analysis of the attached contract. Identify risks for [party: "client" or "contractor"]. Format: Table with columns: - Contract clause (quote the text) - Risk (brief) - Risk level (High/Medium/Low) - Recommendation (how to mitigate) Limitations: - Do not give legal advice — only flags for attention - If unsure about interpretation — explicitly state "Requires specialist review" - Indicate which standard clauses are missing (force majeure, amendment procedure, etc.) Evaluation: Double-check that all quotes accurately match the contract text.
Draft a protocol of disagreements to a contract
Role: You are a contract law attorney. You prepare documents for negotiations. Task: Based on the attached contract, draft a protocol of disagreements. Context: - Our position: [brief description of what we want to achieve] - Key disputed clauses: [list of clause numbers or topics] - Applicable law: [e.g., "Civil Code of the Russian Federation"] Format: Table — Contract line (as is) | Our position | Justification | Proposed wording Introduction: 1-2 paragraphs about the purpose of the protocol and the general approach to negotiations. Evaluation: Check that the proposed wording is legally correct and does not create new risks.
Prepare an NDA memo for employees
Role: You are an intellectual property lawyer. You explain complex things simply. Task: Write a memo for employees: "What cannot be disclosed under the NDA." Context: - Company type: [IT product / consulting / manufacturing] - Key assets: [code/client base/know-how/financial data] - Tone: friendly but clear. No panic, but with understanding of consequences Format: 1. What is an NDA (2 sentences) 2. What is considered confidential (list with examples) 3. What you can do (green list) 4. What you cannot do (red list) 5. What to do if unsure (action algorithm) 6. Where to go with questions Evaluation: Check that the text does not contradict the current legislation of [specify country].

Marketer
Content, SMM, email marketing
Write a LinkedIn post based on ready material
Role: You are a B2B content marketer. You write posts that generate engagement and leads. Task: Create a LinkedIn post based on the following material: [insert text of case/article/news]. Context: - Target audience: [e.g., "HR directors of mid-sized companies"] - Tone: expert but conversational. No officialese - Post goal: [inform / generate leads / announce a product] - Hashtags: no more than 5, relevant to the topic Format: - Hook (first sentence — catchy) - Main idea (2-3 short paragraphs) - Conclusion / call to action - Hashtags Limitation: Maximum 150 words. The post should be scannable — use paragraphs of 1-2 sentences. Evaluation: Check that the post does not sound like an ad but provides value to the reader.
Create an email campaign for a customer segment
Role: You are an email marketer. Your emails get opened and clicked. Task: Write a chain of 3 emails for the segment: [segment description, e.g., "customers inactive for 90 days"]. Context: - Product: [brief description] - Offer: [what we offer] - Tone: [warm/business/playful] - Length: each email — up to 150 words Format for each email: - Subject line (3 options to choose from) - Preview text - Email body - CTA (button/link with text) Limitations: - Email 1: reminder of value - Email 2: social proof or case study - Email 3: last chance + offer Evaluation: Check that subject lines don't trigger spam (no words like "FREE", "URGENT" in all caps).
Develop a content calendar plan for a month
Role: You are a content strategist. You plan calendars that work for the funnel. Task: Create a content plan for [month] for [channel: LinkedIn / blog / Telegram]. Context: - Topic: [e.g., "AI for business"] - Target audience: [description] - Publishing frequency: [e.g., "3 times a week"] - Formats: [posts/cases/polls/videos] Format: Table — Date | Topic | Format | Goal (awareness/leads/engagement) | Call to action | Status Additional: - 3 ideas for "viral" posts - 2 topics for collaborations with experts - 1 repurposing suggestion (how to use one content piece in 3 formats) Evaluation: Check that topics cover different funnel stages (not just sales).

Financial Analyst / FP&A
Analysis, reporting, forecasting
Make an executive summary from a long report
Role: You are a financial analyst. You prepare summaries for a CEO who has 5 minutes. Task: Turn the attached report into a 1-page executive summary. Context: - Audience: [CEO / investors / bank] - What matters: [key metric/event] - Tone: direct, no fluff, numbers in the first paragraph Format: 1. Main conclusion (1-2 sentences, with a key number) 2. 3 key metrics (actual vs plan vs forecast) 3. What went well (2-3 items) 4. What requires attention (2-3 items with recommendations) 5. Next steps (3 actions with responsible parties and deadlines) Limitation: No more than 400 words. Each item — maximum 2 sentences. Evaluation: Check that all numbers exactly match the original report. Do not round without notation.
Analyze budget variances (variance analysis)
Role: You are an FP&A analyst. You find reasons for deviations and propose actions. Task: Analyze actual vs budget deviations for [period]. Data: [insert table: line item | budget | actual | variance | variance %] Format: Table — Line Item | Variance | Likely Cause | Recommendation | Responsible Requirements: - Focus on deviations > [threshold, e.g., 10% or 500K RUB] - For each line item — no more than 1 assumed cause - Recommendation must be specific (not "look into it" but "negotiate with supplier X by [date]") Evaluation: Check that deviations are calculated correctly and causes are logical.
Prepare a commentary on revenue forecast
Role: You are a CFO. You explain numbers so that the business understands. Task: Write a commentary on the revenue forecast for [quarter/year]. Context: - Forecast: [amount] - Base scenario: [assumptions] - Optimistic scenario: [assumptions] - Pessimistic scenario: [assumptions] - Key drivers: [growth factors] - Risks: [main ones] Format: 1. Overall conclusion (2 sentences) 2. Base scenario: logic and key drivers 3. Sensitivity: how will the result change if [factor] changes by ±10% 4. Risks and mitigations 5. What we need from the business (data requests/decisions) Tone: analytical but accessible. No financial jargon. Evaluation: Check that scenarios are internally consistent and drivers logically lead to the result.

Accountant
Bookkeeping, reporting, month-end close
Create a month-end close checklist
Role: You are a chief accountant. You know all the steps of month-end closing by heart. Task: Create a month-end closing checklist for [company type, e.g., "trading company on general taxation system"]. Context: - Accounting system: [1C / SAP / other] - Features: [has import/export / many counterparties / complex cost of goods sold] - Closing deadline: [e.g., "5 business days"] Format: Table — Day | Procedure | Responsible | Control indicator (what we check) | Common errors Requirements: - Indicate the sequence (what comes first: depreciation or currency revaluation?) - Highlight critical steps that everything else depends on - Add a column "If it doesn't balance — what to do" Evaluation: Check that the sequence is logical and nothing is missed.
Analyze fluctuations in a report
Role: You are an accountant-analyst. You find anomalies in numbers. Task: Analyze the attached report and find significant deviations. Context: - Report type: [trial balance / turnover statement / ledger] - Comparison periods: [current month vs last / current vs same month last year] - Materiality threshold: [e.g., "100 thousand RUB or 10%"] Format: Table — Line Item | Current Period | Previous Period | Variance | % | Likely Cause | Needs check? Requirements: - First sort by absolute deviation (largest on top) - For each item > threshold — suggest a cause - Explicitly mark: "Requires clarification from the responsible party" or "Normal fluctuation" Evaluation: Check that deviation calculations are correct.
Check compliance of journal entries with the standard
Role: You are an accounting methodologist. You know [standard: RAS / IFRS / US GAAP]. Task: Check whether the attached journal entries comply with the standard requirements. Context: - Standard: [specify] - Transaction: [description of business transaction] - Entries: [Debit | Credit | Amount | Comment] Format: Table — Entry | Correct/Error | If error — what's wrong | Correct variant | Reference to standard (clause/article) Limitations: - If unsure — write "Requires methodologist consultation" - Do not give legal advice - Indicate which documents should support the transaction Evaluation: Check that references to the standard are current (if referring to a specific clause).

Sales Manager
Proposals, negotiations, CRM
Prepare a commercial proposal
Role: You are a B2B sales manager. You write proposals that close deals. Task: Create a commercial proposal for [client company]. Context: - Our product: [description] - Client need: [from correspondence/call] - Client budget: [range, if known] - Timeline: [if known] - Competitors: [who else the client is considering, if known] Format: 1. Salutation (personalized) 2. Understanding of the client's task (show you listened) 3. Solution (how our product solves the task) 4. Proposal details (list + prices) 5. Implementation timeline 6. Next step (specific CTA) Tone: confident but not pushy. Focus on value, not features. Evaluation: Check that the proposal addresses a specific client pain point, not a template.
Write a follow-up email after a meeting
Role: You are a sales manager. Your emails get replies. Task: Write a follow-up after a meeting with [client name/position]. Context: - Meeting topic: [what was discussed] - Key points: [3-5 items] - Our commitments: [what we promised to send/do] - Client commitments: [what the client promised] - Next step: [specific] - Tone: [business/friendly] Format: - Thanks for the time (1 sentence) - Brief meeting summary (3-4 bullet points) - What we do (with dates) - What we expect from the client (with dates) - Suggestion for next contact Limitation: No more than 200 words. Clients don't read long emails. Evaluation: Check that all dates and commitments match the meeting minutes.
Analyze the sales funnel
Role: You are a sales analyst. You find bottlenecks in the funnel. Task: Analyze the sales funnel for [period]. Data: [insert table: stage | quantity | conversion | average deal size | cycle] Format: 1. Overall picture (2-3 sentences) 2. Table — Stage | Conversion | Problem (yes/no) | Hypothesis for cause | Recommendation 3. Top 3 priorities for improvement 4. Forecast: what happens if we improve conversion by [X]% at each stage Requirements: - Compare with the previous period (if data is available) - Indicate at which stage the most money is lost - Recommendations must be specific, not "need more leads" Evaluation: Check that conversion calculations are correct and priorities are financially justified.

Project Manager
Planning, risks, communications
Create a project plan with resources and risks
Role: You are a project manager with experience in [industry: IT/construction/marketing]. Task: Create a project plan: [project name]. Context: - Project goal: [SMART goal] - Budget: [amount] - Timeline: [start date — finish date] - Team: [roles and headcount] - Key constraints: [e.g., "cannot stop current sales"] Format: 1. WBS (work breakdown structure) — list of phases with subtasks 2. Table — Task | Responsible | Deadline | Dependencies | Status 3. Risks — table: Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation | Risk owner 4. Critical path (3-5 tasks that the deadline depends on) 5. Checkpoints (what we check at each stage) Evaluation: Check that the plan is realistic (not 3 tasks in 1 day for one person).
Conduct a sprint/project retrospective
Role: You are a Scrum Master. You run retrospectives after which the team grows. Task: Prepare a retrospective template for [team/project] for [period]. Context: - What was planned: [goals/commitments] - What worked: [facts, metrics] - What didn't work: [problems] - Team composition: [roles] Format: 1. Sprint dashboard (3-4 numbers: planned/completed, velocity, bugs) 2. What went well (3 items with specifics, not "team is great") 3. What can be improved (3 items with root cause) 4. Experiments for the next sprint (2-3 actions with owners and success metrics) 5. Kudos (place for colleagues) Tone: constructive, without blame. Evaluation: Check that actions from item 4 are measurable and assigned to specific people.
Compose a project status update report
Role: You are a project manager. You write status updates that get read. Task: Prepare a weekly status update for [audience: client/management/team]. Context: - Project: [name] - Current phase: [name] - Progress: [% completed] - Budget: [spent/total] - Key achievements this week: [2-3 items] - Blockers: [what's in the way] - Help needed: [from whom and on what] Format: 1. Traffic light (🟢/🟡/🔴) across 4 dimensions: schedule, budget, quality, risks 2. What was done (3-5 items) 3. What is planned (3-5 items with dates) 4. Blockers and help needed 5. Key decisions that need to be made Limitation: No more than 1 page. Management doesn't read long reports. Evaluation: Check that the status is objective — no hidden problems behind a green light.

Recruiter
Sourcing, interviews, candidate assessment
Create behavioral interview questions
Role: You are an IT recruiter. You create questions that predict success on the job. Task: Create 8 behavioral interview questions for the position [position]. Context: - Key competencies: [list: e.g., "conflict management", "task decomposition", "working in uncertainty"] - Level: [junior/middle/senior/lead] - Interview format: [45 minutes / 1 hour] - Company: [brief description of culture] Format: Table — Competency | Question | What to listen for in the answer | Red flags Requirements: - Questions using the STAR method (Situation-Task-Action-Result) - 1-2 questions per competency - Add 1-2 questions for cultural fit Evaluation: Check that questions are open-ended (not "yes/no") and measure what matters for the role.
Analyze a candidate's resume
Role: You are a recruiter. You analyze resumes for fit with the vacancy. Task: Compare the candidate's resume with the vacancy requirements. Context: - Vacancy: [position, key requirements] - Resume: [insert resume text or key points] Format: Table — Requirement | Match (Yes/No/Partial) | Evidence from resume | Questions for the interview Additional: - Overall fit assessment (%) - Candidate's strengths (2-3 items) - Potential risks (2-3 items) - Recommendation: "Invite" / "Invite with reservations" / "Reject" Evaluation: Check that the assessment is objective and based only on resume data (not assumptions).
Write a rejection email to a candidate (preserving reputation)
Role: You are an HR manager. You can say "no" in a way that makes the person respect the company. Task: Write a rejection letter to candidate [name]. Context: - Position: [name] - Stage: [at which stage the rejection — after resume/interview/test task] - Reason: [lacks experience in X / chose another / client's decision] - Candidate: [brief description, why they are good even if they didn't pass] Format: - Thanks for the interest (1 sentence) - The decision (honest but tactful) - What we valued in the candidate (specifics — not generic phrases) - Feedback, if appropriate (1 constructive point) - Invitation to stay in touch (if the candidate is strong) Tone: respectful, warm. No templates like "we will keep your resume on file." Evaluation: Read from the candidate's perspective. If you received such a letter — what feelings would it evoke?

PR Manager
Press releases, crisis communications, media
Write a press release about an event
Role: You are a PR manager. You write press releases that media publish. Task: Create a press release on the topic: [event, e.g., "product launch / fundraising / partnership"]. Context: - Company: [name, description of activities] - News: [what happened, facts, numbers] - Quote from the leader: [key thought, if available] - Target media: [business/tech/mass] Format (standard press release): 1. Headline (informative, no hype) 2. Lead (who, what, when, where, why — 1 paragraph) 3. Main text (2-3 paragraphs with facts) 4. Quote (1 paragraph) 5. About the company (boilerplate) 6. Press contacts Limitations: - No more than 500 words - No superlatives ("best", "first", "revolutionary") — unless there is proof - All numbers and facts are verifiable Evaluation: Check that the lead answers 5W1H and contains the main news.
Prepare a response to negative coverage in social media/media
Role: You are a crisis communications PR manager. You work quickly and precisely. Task: Prepare a response to a publication/comment. Context: - Source: [where published, authority] - Essence of the claim: [briefly] - Facts: [what actually happened] - Our position: [what we are ready to admit/what we dispute] - Audience: [clients/partners/general public] Format: 1. Quick assessment: threat level (low/medium/high) and why 2. 3 response options: - Option A: diplomatic, soft - Option B: direct, with facts - Option C: brief, without emotion 3. Recommendation: which option to choose and why 4. Next steps (what we do after publishing the response) Limitation: No more than 150 words per response. In a crisis, brevity is the soul of wit. Evaluation: Check that the response does not sound defensive and does not add fuel to the fire.
Create a communication plan for a launch
Role: You are a PR strategist. You plan communications that achieve goals. Task: Create a communication plan for the launch of [product/event]. Context: - Launch date: [when] - Target audience: [who] - Key message: [1 sentence] - Channels: [which we use] - Budget: [if limited] Format: Table — Stage | Date | Channel | Format | Message | Responsible | KPI Stages: - Pre-launch (2 weeks before): teasers, announcement - Launch day: press release, posts, newsletter - Post-launch (1-2 weeks): case studies, testimonials, results Additional: - Message matrix: what we say to different audiences - Plan B: what to do if the launch is delayed Evaluation: Check that the plan is realistic in terms of timelines and resources.

Business Analyst
Market analysis, processes, requirements
Conduct a SWOT analysis of a company/product
Role: You are a business analyst. You create SWOTs that drive decisions. Task: Create a SWOT analysis for [company/product/direction]. Context: - Industry: [name] - Size: [revenue, employees, geography] - Key products: [list] - Competitors: [3-5 main ones] - Market: [trends, dynamics] Format: 2×2 table — Strengths | Weaknesses | Opportunities | Threats Requirements: - In each cell — 4-5 items, sorted by impact - Each item — specific, with an explanation of "why" - Strengths and Weaknesses — internal factors - Opportunities and Threats — external factors Additional: - 2 SO strategies (use strengths for opportunities) - 2 WT strategies (how to neutralize weaknesses and threats) Evaluation: Check that factors are objective and based on data, not assumptions.
Create business requirements for a feature (BRD)
Role: You are a business analyst. You write requirements that developers implement without questions. Task: Create a business requirements document for the feature: [feature name]. Context: - Product: [name] - User: [who will use it] - Problem: [what's wrong now] - Expected result: [how it will be after implementation] - Constraints: [timelines, budget, integrations] Format: 1. General description (2-3 paragraphs) 2. Business goals (SMART) 3. User scenarios (2-3 scenarios in the format "As a [role], I want [action], so that [goal]") 4. Functional requirements (list with priorities: Must/Should/Could) 5. Non-functional requirements (performance, security, scalability) 6. Acceptance criteria (when is the task considered complete) 7. Open questions Evaluation: Check that requirements are unambiguous and verifiable (no words like "convenient", "fast" without numbers).
Analyze the market of suppliers/competitors
Role: You are a market analyst. You collect data and structure it for decision-making. Task: Compare [suppliers / competitors / solutions] for [company's task]. Context: - What we are looking for: [e.g., "CRM system for a B2B company of 100 people"] - Criteria: [list: price, features, integrations, support, implementation timeline] - Criteria weights: [if available] - Budget: [range] Format: 1. Comparison table — Criterion | Option A | Option B | Option C | Winner 2. Detailed breakdown by each criterion (2-3 sentences) 3. Hidden risks (what can go wrong with each option) 4. Recommendation with justification 5. Next steps (what to do to make a decision) Limitations: - Do not use data older than [year] if it concerns prices - If unsure about a number — indicate "Requires clarification" - Indicate sources, if available Evaluation: Check that the comparison is objective and across all criteria.

Procurement Specialist
Tenders, negotiations, contracts
Compare supplier commercial proposals
Role: You are a procurement specialist. You analyze proposals to choose the best offer. Task: Compare commercial proposals from [number] suppliers. Data: [insert table: supplier | price | timeline | payment terms | warranties | additional services] Context: - What we are purchasing: [description] - Critical factors: [e.g., "delivery time is more important than price by 20%"] - Constraints: [budget, mandatory certification, etc.] Format: Table — Criterion | Weight | Supplier A | Supplier B | Supplier C | Winner Results: - 1st-2nd-3rd place with justification - Recommendation: with whom to negotiate - What else needs to be clarified from the winner Evaluation: Check that the comparison covers all criteria and weights are applied correctly.
Check delivery contract terms
Role: You are a procurement specialist with a legal eye. You check contracts before signing. Task: Analyze the terms of a delivery contract. Context: - Our role: [buyer / seller] - Goods/service: [description] - Typical risks: [e.g., "delivery delay", "poor quality goods", "price change"] Format: Table — Clause | What is written | Norm/Risk | Recommendation Key clauses to check: - Price and amendment procedure - Delivery timelines and liability for delay - Payment terms (prepayment/postpayment/letter of credit) - Risk transfer (Incoterms) - Warranties and claims procedure - Force majeure and dispute resolution Evaluation: Check that there are no disproportionate risks for our side.
Create a purchase request (specification for supplier)
Role: You are a procurement engineer. You write specifications that suppliers give exact prices for. Task: Create a technical specification for the procurement of [goods/services]. Context: - What we are purchasing: [detailed description] - Volume: [quantity, frequency] - Requirements: [technical/quality/logistics] - Standards: [GOST/ISO/specifications] Format: 1. General information (about the company, about the procurement) 2. Technical requirements (list with tolerances) 3. Packaging and labeling requirements 4. Logistics (delivery terms, timelines, address) 5. Documentation (which documents are needed: certificates, passports) 6. Acceptance and claims procedure 7. Supplier response form (what they must indicate in the response) Limitations: - Do not specify specific brands (unless critical) - Use measurable requirements (not "high quality" but "content of X at least 99%") Evaluation: Check that the specification contains no contradictions and all requirements are verifiable.

Account Manager
Client support, retention, upsell
Prepare a meeting plan with a key client
Role: You are an account manager. You prepare meetings after which the client wants to continue working. Task: Create a meeting plan with client [company name]. Context: - Meeting type: [regular / QBR / problem-focused / expansion] - History: [how long we've worked together, what they bought, support tickets] - Client participants: [roles] - Meeting goal: [what we want to achieve] Format: 1. Agenda (timing by items) 2. For each item: goal, key questions, what information to prepare 3. Meeting materials (which reports/presentations to bring) 4. Anticipated objections and responses 5. Next step (what we lead the meeting to) Limitation: Meeting up to 60 minutes. The agenda must fit. Evaluation: Check that the agenda is oriented toward value for the client, not just our benefit.
Write an email proposing expansion of cooperation
Role: You are an account manager. You propose more in a way that the client thanks you. Task: Write an email to client [name] proposing to expand cooperation. Context: - What the client already uses: [product/service] - Results: [specific numbers, if available] - What we propose: [new product/module/volume] - Benefit for the client: [savings/growth/risk reduction] Format: - Reminder of current results (1 paragraph with numbers) - Observation/insight ("We noticed that...") - Proposal (brief, with benefit) - Social proof ("Client X already uses it and got...") - Call to action (specific next step) Tone: partnership-based, not pushy. The client is already with us — no need to "sell." Evaluation: Read from the client's perspective. Does this look like care for their business or a sales pitch?
Analyze client satisfaction (based on data)
Role: You are a client experience analyst. You find patterns in client data. Task: Analyze data for client [name]. Data: [insert: NPS, support tickets, feature usage, expansions/reductions, dates] Format: 1. Overall client health assessment (🟢/🟡/🔴) 2. Table — Metric | Current | Dynamics | Assessment 3. Signals (2-3 observations that may indicate risk or opportunity) 4. Recommendations: - What to do in the next 30 days - What to propose (upsell/training/meeting) 5. Who from the team should take action Evaluation: Check that conclusions are based on data, not intuition.

⚡ How to Adapt to Your Task

Each prompt above is a template. To get the best result:

  • Replace everything in square brackets [like this] with your data
  • Add context — the more details about your company/situation, the more accurate the answer
  • Use few-shot — attach 1-2 examples of how you want the result to look
  • Ask to rephrase — if the result isn't perfect, ask to "make it shorter", "add numbers", "rewrite for [audience]"
  • Always verify — AI helps prepare, but responsibility for the result remains with you
