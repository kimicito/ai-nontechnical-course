Level 15 — Legal Function

15.1 Internal Tasks: On-Prem

⏱ 25 min · for lawyers, corporate secretaries, compliance specialists
Closed Loop (On-Prem)
All tasks in this lesson work with attorney-client privilege, insider information, personal data, company strategic plans. Use internal model or local LLM.

Why On-Prem is Critical for Lawyers

The legal function is one of the most closed by default. Attorney-client privilege, insider information about deals and disputes — all of this requires strict access control. If case materials or negotiation position on a dispute are requested by a court or regulator, using an external cloud service may raise questions about confidentiality breach and affect privilege defense in some jurisdictions.

Default rule: if material is related to a specific dispute, deal, counterparty or employee — on-prem only. To the external environment, only work with open legal field can be taken.

When On-Prem is Mandatory:
  • Review of incoming contracts and preparation of amendments (real commercial terms, negotiation strategy)
  • Due diligence of counterparties and M&A deals (insider information, leak = market manipulation)
  • Preparation of statements of claim, responses, appeals (attorney-client privilege, defense strategy)
  • Formation of legal position on dispute (role-playing witness/opponent examination)
  • Preparation of evidence base, case document systematization (specific dispute)
  • Pre-trial correspondence on real cases (specific amounts, violations, demands)
  • Corporate documents (minutes, resolutions) — management information before publication
  • Sanctions compliance screening for specific counterparties/deals
  • Responses to regulator inquiries (strategically sensitive)
  • Legal opinions on specific business issues (real facts, amounts, risks)
  • Labor disputes, employer position (personal data, potential conflict)
  • Contract registry management (full list of commercial relations)
  • Reorganizations and creation of subsidiaries (strategically sensitive)

1. Incoming Contract Review

Comparing incoming contract with corporate standard, identifying risky provisions, preparing amendments.

Prompt Example (On-Prem)
Review incoming contract against company standards:

Incoming contract (text):
[PASTE CONTRACT TEXT]

Company standard (key provisions):
- Supplier liability: not less than [SPECIFY]% of shipment value
- Payment term: no earlier than [SPECIFY] days from delivery date
- Force majeure: according to [SPECIFY LEGISLATION]
- Pre-trial procedure: [SPECIFY TERM] days
- Arbitration: [SPECIFY PLACE]

Provide:
1. List of deviations from standard (with clause references)
2. Risk assessment for each deviation (high/medium/low)
3. Proposed amendments (text)
4. Arguments for negotiations with counterparty
5. Alternative formulations (compromise)

Result: reviewed contract with amendments, saves 2–3 hours.

Knowledge Check

Question 1: Why is statement of claim preparation on-prem only?

Because it contains attorney-client privilege, dispute strategy and confidential facts
Only if claim amount exceeds a certain threshold
Can be done in cloud if party names are anonymized

Question 2: Can sanctions screening be done in cloud?

Yes, sanctions lists are public data
No — interpretation of results for specific counterparty and deal is internal information
Hybrid — lists in cloud, decision inside

Question 3: Why are responses to regulator inquiries on-prem?

Because special response form is required
Because they reveal company strategic position and may affect ongoing disputes
Only if response is required within 24 hours
